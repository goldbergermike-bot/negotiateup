**No-Cliff Day-1 Liquidity Strategy**

Workday RSUs vest quarterly with NO cliff from Day 1. Your first paycheck includes RSU value. At a FAANG company with a 1-year cliff, you see $0 in equity for 12 months. A Workday RSU grant of $300K with Day-1 vesting delivers $18.75K in real liquidity in Q1 — while a $360K grant at a cliff company delivers $0 for the first year. When comparing offers, calculate the "liquidity-adjusted" value: Workday's grant delivers faster real-world value.

At the Senior level, this becomes particularly powerful. A Workday RSU grant of $260K with Day-1 quarterly vesting is worth more in real liquidity than a $312K grant at a cliff company where you see nothing for 12 months. In Year 1, Workday delivers $65K in vested equity across four quarters. The cliff competitor delivers $0 until month 13. Frame this directly: "I'm accepting a lower RSU total because Workday's Day-1 vesting puts real money in my account from Q1."

**Global Levers**

1. **Day-1 Liquidity vs. Cliff Competitor:** "I have a competing offer at $300K+ RSU with a 1-year cliff. Workday's Day-1 quarterly vesting is worth more to me in real terms — but I need the RSU grant at $290K+ to close the gap. Can we get to $290K over 4 years?"

3. **Triple-Lever Optimization:** Workday's balanced model lets you optimize across three axes. If equity is at the band ceiling, push base: "I understand RSU is at the top of band. Can we bridge the remaining gap with a base increase to $222K?" If base is capped, push for a guaranteed Year 1 bonus: "Can we guarantee my first-year bonus at 100% of the 15% target?"
4. **Retention Signal Leverage:** Workday's RSU refresh grants reward retention. Ask: "What does the typical refresh grant look like for a strong-performing Senior SWE after Year 1?" Use the answer to calibrate your initial ask — if refreshes are generous, you can accept a slightly lower initial grant.
