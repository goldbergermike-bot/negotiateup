### Role-Specific Technical Depth

The Hospitality Intelligence Platform Engineer role requires expertise across four technical domains that define the platform architecture:

**1. Data Fusion and Pipeline Architecture**

The platform ingests data from multiple sources — Toast's POS terminals, payment processors, kitchen display systems, workforce management tools, and now Instacart's delivery platform. Each source has different schemas, latency characteristics, and reliability profiles. The platform engineer designs the ingestion layer (likely Kafka-based), the transformation layer (Flink or Spark Streaming), and the serving layer (a combination of real-time feature stores and analytical databases). The Feb 10 2026 Instacart Partnership specifically requires building an event-driven integration architecture that can handle schema evolution across two independently versioned platforms while maintaining data freshness guarantees measured in seconds, not minutes.

**2. ML Serving Infrastructure**

Toast IQ's intelligence products depend on ML models being served at scale — demand forecasting models that predict next-hour restaurant traffic, menu optimization models that recommend price adjustments, and staffing models that suggest shift schedules. The platform engineer builds the model serving infrastructure: model versioning, A/B testing frameworks, feature stores, prediction caching, and monitoring/alerting for model drift. This infrastructure must serve predictions to 120,000+ restaurant locations with latency requirements that make intelligence insights feel instantaneous to restaurant operators checking their Toast IQ dashboard during a busy dinner service.

**3. Analytics Engine and Query Layer**

Beyond ML models, Toast IQ serves structured analytics — revenue dashboards, comparative benchmarks, trend analysis, and operational KPIs. The platform engineer designs the analytics engine that powers these experiences: choosing the query engine (e.g., ClickHouse, Druid, or a managed solution), designing the data model for multi-tenant analytics at scale, and building the API layer that front-end applications consume. The cross-channel analytics enabled by the Instacart partnership require a query architecture that can join real-time delivery data with in-restaurant transaction data without sacrificing performance.

**4. Platform Extensibility and Developer Experience**

As Toast IQ matures, it will need to support custom intelligence applications — both internal teams building new insights products and, eventually, external partners building on Toast's intelligence platform. The platform engineer designs the extensibility layer: APIs, SDKs, data access patterns, and security models that enable this expansion. This is the long-term architectural bet that determines whether Toast IQ becomes a platform or remains a product — and the Hospitality Intelligence Platform Engineer makes the foundational decisions.

### Compensation Negotiation Playbook

Given the signature importance of this role, here is a detailed negotiation playbook:

**Opening Position:** Always open 15-20% above the top of the published range. For Boston, open at $260,000 base + $280,000 RSU/4yr + $55,000 bonus = $403,000 total comp. This anchors the negotiation high and gives room to "concede" to your actual target.

**Target Position:** After back-and-forth, target: Boston $240,000 base + $240,000 RSU/4yr + $48,000 bonus = $368,000. New York $255,000 base + $270,000 RSU/4yr + $52,000 bonus = $400,000. San Francisco $270,000 base + $300,000 RSU/4yr + $58,000 bonus = $433,000.

**Concession Strategy:** Concede on bonus first (it is the smallest component), hold firm on base, and negotiate hardest on equity. Toast's Hospitality Intelligence growth thesis means RSUs are the highest-leverage component — argue that Toast IQ's revenue contribution will drive stock price appreciation, making RSUs worth more than their face value.

**Signing Bonus:** If the RSU number is firm, push for a $35,000-$50,000 signing bonus, framing it as a "bridge" for the value lost during the first-year vesting cliff. Cite the urgency of the Instacart partnership integration as the reason Toast should want you to start immediately rather than wait for a competing offer to materialize.

**Refreshers:** Negotiate annual RSU refreshers of $40,000-$60,000 starting in Year 2. Frame this as alignment with Toast IQ's expected revenue ramp — as the platform generates more subscription revenue, your equity should grow proportionally.

**Accept-At Floor:** Do not accept below: $210,000 base with $180,000 RSUs in Boston ($290,000 total); $225,000 base with $200,000 RSUs in New York ($318,000 total); $240,000 base with $220,000 RSUs in San Francisco ($343,000 total).
