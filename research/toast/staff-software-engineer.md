**Negotiation DNA**

Staff Software Engineers at Toast operate as the company's technical strategists — setting architectural direction, owning cross-team technical initiatives, and making decisions that shape the platform serving 120,000+ restaurant locations. At this level, you are not just writing code; you are defining the systems that underpin Toast's transformation from a payments-and-POS company into a full Hospitality Intelligence platform. The Feb 10 2026 Instacart Partnership exemplifies the caliber of work at this level: staff engineers are designing the integration architecture that merges Toast's restaurant operating system with Instacart's delivery infrastructure, creating a technical foundation that neither company could build alone.

Toast IQ — the company's AI-powered restaurant intelligence product — represents the strategic frontier where staff engineers have the most leverage. Building the data architecture, model serving infrastructure, and real-time analytics pipelines that power Toast IQ requires deep systems expertise combined with product intuition. Toast's $4B+ revenue and $20B+ market cap create a compensation environment where staff engineers are paid competitively with FAANG-adjacent companies, but the Hospitality Intelligence growth thesis means RSU grants carry asymmetric upside. Staff engineers should negotiate aggressively on equity, recognizing that Toast's transformation into an intelligence platform is still in its early innings.

The competitive landscape for staff-level talent is intense. Toast competes directly with Stripe, Block, Shopify, and cloud infrastructure companies for engineers who can operate at this altitude. Candidates with experience in large-scale distributed systems, payments infrastructure, ML platform engineering, or restaurant/hospitality technology command premium offers.

> **Negotiate Up Strategy:** Anchor to the 80th percentile and negotiate equity first. Push RSU grants by 25-35% above initial offer, citing Toast IQ's strategic importance and staff-level architectural ownership. For Boston, target $245,000 base + $300,000 RSU/4yr + $48,000 bonus = $372,000 total comp. For San Francisco, target $275,000 base + $380,000 RSU/4yr + $58,000 bonus = $438,000 total comp. Accept-at floor: $225,000 base with $240,000 RSUs in Boston; $240,000 base with $280,000 RSUs in New York; $255,000 base with $300,000 RSUs in San Francisco. If equity is capped, negotiate a $40,000-$60,000 signing bonus and request an accelerated first-year RSU vest (e.g., 33% Year 1 instead of 25%). Staff-level candidates should always present competing offers — Toast's recruiting team expects it at this level.
