**Negotiation DNA**

DevOps Engineers at Toast are the operational backbone of a platform where downtime directly translates to lost revenue for 120,000+ restaurant locations. When Toast's infrastructure falters, restaurants cannot process payments, accept orders, or manage their operations — making reliability a revenue-critical concern, not merely a technical metric. The platform processes billions of dollars in transactions annually, and DevOps engineers are responsible for the CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure-as-code, monitoring systems, and deployment strategies that keep this massive system running with sub-second latency and near-perfect uptime.

The Feb 10 2026 Instacart Partnership has dramatically increased infrastructure complexity. DevOps engineers must now ensure seamless connectivity between Toast's restaurant operating system and Instacart's delivery platform — managing cross-cloud integrations, API gateway reliability, and data synchronization across two large-scale production environments. Simultaneously, the launch of Toast IQ — the company's AI-powered Hospitality Intelligence platform — introduces new infrastructure demands: ML model serving, real-time analytics pipelines, and GPU-accelerated compute workloads that require specialized DevOps expertise. Toast's $4B+ revenue and $20B+ market cap reflect a company that understands infrastructure investment is foundational to growth.
