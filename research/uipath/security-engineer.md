**Negotiation DNA**
Security Engineers at UiPath protect a platform that has privileged access to enterprise systems worldwide. UiPath's software robots operate with credentials that allow them to access ERP systems, financial databases, CRM platforms, and other critical enterprise applications. A security breach at UiPath could expose bot credentials that provide access to thousands of enterprises' most sensitive systems. This extraordinary security surface creates genuine leverage for security professionals who can protect the platform and its credential management infrastructure.

UiPath compensates Security Engineers at a premium over standard engineering roles (10-15% higher), reflecting the critical nature of automation security and the scarcity of security professionals who understand the unique security challenges of RPA platforms. The company maintains SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and industry-specific certifications, and security engineers are responsible for evolving these as the platform grows.

The security challenges at UiPath are distinctive: managing and protecting robot credentials (the Credential Store), securing the Orchestrator API, ensuring secure bot execution in customer environments, and protecting the AI Center from adversarial attacks. Security engineers who understand the intersection of enterprise security and automation platforms occupy a specialized niche.
