#### Negotiation DNA

DevOps Engineers at Blackstone own the infrastructure reliability and deployment pipelines for systems that process, monitor, and report on $1.3 trillion in assets under management. This is not a typical DevOps role — when your CI/CD pipeline fails or your Kubernetes cluster has a node failure, the downstream impact is measured in billions of dollars of delayed deal execution, missed capital calls, or unavailable investor reporting. Blackstone's technology infrastructure has undergone a massive cloud-native transformation, migrating from legacy on-premise data centers to a hybrid multi-cloud architecture spanning AWS, Azure, and private cloud environments. DevOps Engineers at Blackstone manage this hybrid infrastructure while maintaining the security, compliance, and auditability standards required by SEC, FINRA, and global financial regulators. The firm's 24/7 global operations — spanning New York, London, Hong Kong, Sydney, and Mumbai — demand infrastructure that never sleeps. NYSE: BX equity provides liquid RSU compensation, while perpetual capital fee streams fund stable, predictable bonuses.

#### AI Infrastructure Buildout — The Perpetual Capital Premium

DevOps Engineers at Blackstone have a unique dual relationship with AI infrastructure. Externally, Blackstone is the world's largest private investor in AI infrastructure — deploying $100B+ into data centers, power generation, and fiber networks. DevOps Engineers build and maintain the internal systems that Blackstone's infrastructure investment team uses to monitor these physical assets: uptime dashboards for data center portfolios, automated alerts for power grid disruptions, and deployment pipelines for the analytics platforms that track construction progress across dozens of global sites.

Internally, Blackstone's own AI and ML workloads are growing rapidly — model training infrastructure, GPU cluster management, feature store pipelines, and inference serving systems all fall within the DevOps team's purview. You are building the internal infrastructure that enables Blackstone's data scientists and ML engineers to deploy models at scale.

The perpetual capital premium for DevOps Engineers is rooted in infrastructure permanence. Perpetual capital vehicles (40%+ of AUM, $4B+ in annual fees) require always-on, zero-downtime platforms — investor portals, NAV calculation systems, and automated distribution engines that must operate continuously for the life of the vehicle (which is, by definition, perpetual). This creates a structurally stable demand for DevOps talent and a bonus pool funded by recurring management fees.

**Negotiation lever:** *"I'll maintain the infrastructure for platforms serving perpetual capital vehicles with $500B+ in AUM — systems that must operate 24/7/365 with zero tolerance for downtime. The permanence of these vehicles and their fee revenue justifies a guaranteed Year 1 bonus of $65K."*
