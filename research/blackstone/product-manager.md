#### Negotiation DNA

Product Managers at Blackstone define and deliver the technology products that power the world's largest alternative asset manager. With $1.3 trillion in AUM across private equity, real estate, credit, and infrastructure, PMs at Blackstone own product strategy for platforms that serve three critical audiences: investment professionals executing billion-dollar deals, operations teams managing 250+ portfolio companies, and institutional LPs (sovereign wealth funds, pensions, endowments) who demand institutional-grade reporting and transparency. This is a fundamentally different PM role than big tech — your product decisions directly impact how capital is deployed, how risk is managed, and how $1.3T in assets is monitored in real time. Blackstone's technology transformation has elevated the PM function from a support role to a strategic position, with PMs sitting in investment committee meetings and shaping the firm's digital roadmap. The combination of NYSE: BX equity, perpetual capital-funded bonuses, and the prestige of the Blackstone brand creates a compelling package for PMs who want to operate at the intersection of technology and finance.

#### AI Infrastructure Buildout — The Perpetual Capital Premium

Product Managers at Blackstone are increasingly focused on AI infrastructure-related products. As the firm deploys $100B+ into data centers, power generation, and fiber networks, PMs own the product roadmap for the platforms that investment professionals use to source, evaluate, and monitor these investments. This includes AI-powered deal sourcing tools that scan thousands of potential data center sites, automated financial modeling platforms that project energy costs and capacity utilization, and LP reporting dashboards that provide real-time visibility into infrastructure portfolio performance.

The perpetual capital premium for PMs is direct and measurable. Over 40% of Blackstone's AUM sits in perpetual capital vehicles, generating $4B+ in stable, recurring management fees. PMs who build products for these vehicles — including the digital platforms for BREIT (real estate), BCRED (credit), and BIP (infrastructure) — are building for a permanent user base with a permanent revenue stream. This means your product roadmap is not subject to fund lifecycle constraints; you are building products that will be used for decades. Your bonus, funded by these management fees, is structurally more stable than at any traditional fund.

**Negotiation lever:** *"I'll be owning product strategy for platforms that serve perpetual capital vehicles representing $500B+ in AUM. These products generate recurring management fee revenue — my bonus should reflect this permanence with a guaranteed minimum of $80K in Year 1."*
