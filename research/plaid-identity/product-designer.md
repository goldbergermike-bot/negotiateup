**Negotiation DNA**

Product designers at Plaid face a unique challenge: making complex financial infrastructure feel simple. You're designing API documentation portals, identity verification flows, and bank-linking experiences that serve both sophisticated developers and non-technical end users. Plaid's design quality directly impacts developer adoption — a confusing onboarding flow means lost customers, and each lost developer-customer represents thousands of end users who won't connect their bank accounts through Plaid.

Plaid's design team is smaller than engineering, which means each designer owns a disproportionately large surface area. You'll be designing for regulatory-compliant flows (where UI copy has legal implications), cross-cultural financial experiences (international expansion), and trust-critical moments (users sharing bank credentials). This is not standard product design — it's high-stakes design where a poor UX decision can trigger regulatory scrutiny or erode consumer trust. [Source: Plaid Design Team, 2025-2026]

### Identity Verification UX Lever

Identity verification is the most design-sensitive product at Plaid. Users are being asked to photograph government IDs, take selfies, and share sensitive personal information — all within a flow that needs to feel trustworthy, accessible, and fast. The conversion rate of the identity verification flow directly determines product revenue. A 5% improvement in verification completion rate translates to millions in additional revenue.

If you have experience designing trust-critical flows (banking, healthcare, government), you carry a premium. The intersection of beautiful design and regulatory compliance is rare — most designers can do one or the other, but Plaid needs both.

**Global Levers**

1. **Conversion Rate Revenue Impact:** "The identity verification flow's conversion rate directly drives Plaid's revenue. A 5% improvement in completion rate from my design work translates to millions in additional annual revenue. My equity should reflect this direct revenue attribution."
2. **Trust-Critical Design Expertise:** "I design flows where users share government IDs and bank credentials. This trust-critical design expertise — understanding how visual design, copy, and interaction patterns build trust — is rare and directly transferable."
3. **Regulatory-Compliant UX:** "I understand how to design flows where UI copy has legal implications. This regulatory design expertise takes years to develop and Plaid needs it for international expansion."
4. **Surface Area Ownership:** "Plaid's design team is small relative to engineering. I'll own a disproportionately large product surface area, which means my design decisions have outsized impact."

> **Negotiate Up Strategy:** "I'd like the equity grant at $195K over 4 years with a $20K signing bonus. Given the revenue impact of the identity verification flow design, I want the equity sized to reflect the direct conversion rate improvements I'll deliver." Plaid will counter at $155K-$180K equity — accept at $170K+ with the signing bonus.
