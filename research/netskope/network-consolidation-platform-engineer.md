#### Advanced Negotiation Dynamics: Post-IPO Equity

Netskope's September 2025 IPO fundamentally changed the equity negotiation landscape. Pre-IPO, RSU grants were illiquid, speculative, and difficult to value. Post-IPO, Netskope RSUs are publicly traded securities with a known market price, standard liquidity, and transparent vesting schedules.

This shift has several implications for your negotiation:

1. **No more illiquidity discount.** Pre-IPO, candidates could argue for larger grants to compensate for illiquidity risk. Post-IPO, Netskope will counter that RSUs are now "real money" and should be valued at face value. Push back by noting that the stock is still volatile in its early post-IPO trading period and that a premium is warranted for single-stock concentration risk.

2. **Stock appreciation as upside.** With 34% ARR growth, Netskope's stock may appreciate significantly from the IPO price. Frame your RSU ask in terms of shares, not dollar amounts — this locks in your grant size regardless of stock price movements, and positions you to benefit from future appreciation.

3. **Refresher grants matter.** Post-IPO companies typically issue annual RSU refresher grants. Negotiate for clarity on the refresher process and expected grant sizes — a strong initial grant with weak refreshers erodes your total compensation over time.

4. **Vesting schedule leverage.** Standard 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff is the norm, but you can negotiate for accelerated vesting (e.g., monthly after the cliff, or a front-loaded schedule with 40% vesting in Year 1) to increase your near-term liquidity.
