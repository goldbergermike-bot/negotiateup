### Senior Software Engineer — Fly.io Salary Negotiation Guide

**Negotiation DNA**: Senior Software Engineers at Fly.io own major platform subsystems — microVM orchestration, global networking, distributed storage, or developer tools — with the autonomy and impact that comes from a small, deeply technical team.

#### Compensation Benchmarks (2026)

| Level | Chicago (USD) | Remote US (USD) |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Senior (IC3) | $238,000–$318,000 | $220,000–$300,000 |
| Senior+ | $280,000–$358,000 | $263,000–$340,000 |

*Total compensation includes base salary ($170K-$215K), startup equity (options), and potential bonus.*

#### Lever 1: Platform Subsystem Ownership — "At Fly.io's scale, I own entire platform subsystems. My architectural decisions directly shape the product."
#### Lever 2: Startup Impact — "Each senior engineer has outsized impact at a small startup. My contributions are directly visible to customers."
#### Lever 3: Equity Negotiation — "I expect a meaningful equity stake reflecting both my impact and the startup risk I'm taking."
#### Lever 4: Competing Offers — "I'm evaluating offers from well-funded startups and Big Tech. Fly.io's equity upside must compensate for the risk."

> **Negotiate Up Strategy**: Open at $200,000 base with equity valued at $75K-$110K/year. Accept-at floor: $280,000 total comp. Push for larger option grant.
