**Advanced Negotiation Tactics for the Trust Architect Role**

**Tactic 1 — The Competitive Landscape Anchor:** When negotiating, reference specific competitive offers:
- "Google is offering L6 ML Infrastructure roles at $280K base + $400K RSU/4yr for similar AI governance platform work."
- "Stripe's Platform Engineering team has competing offers at $260K base + $350K RSU/4yr."
- "I have an offer from a Series D AI governance startup at $240K base + 1.5% equity."

Use these anchors to establish that $250K+ base is market rate for Trust Architect-caliber talent, and that Tipalti's private equity illiquidity requires a premium on options or supplemental cash.

**Tactic 2 — The 409A Investigation:** Before accepting any options grant, request:
- Current 409A fair market value and date of last valuation
- Latest preferred stock price from most recent funding round
- Total diluted share count and your percentage ownership
- Secondary market transaction prices (if any)
- Estimated time to liquidity event (IPO or acquisition)

Use this data to calculate the realistic value of your options. If the 409A is stale (>6 months old) or the strike price is above secondary market prices, negotiate for additional cash compensation or a larger grant size.

**Tactic 3 — The Trust Architect Career Ladder:** This role is new enough that Tipalti may not have a formal career ladder for it. Use this as negotiation leverage:
- Request a written commitment to defining IC5, IC6, and IC7 Trust Architect levels within 12 months
- Negotiate guaranteed comp review at 12 months with a minimum 10% adjustment if a ladder is established
- Ask for a "role definition bonus" of $20K-$30K for the organizational design work of establishing the Trust Architect discipline

**Tactic 4 — The AI Trust Milestone Bonus:** Propose a milestone-based bonus structure tied to AI Trust outcomes:
