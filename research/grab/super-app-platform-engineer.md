**Negotiation DNA**

Grab is Southeast Asia's leading super-app, serving over 40 million monthly transacting users across eight countries (Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines, Vietnam, Cambodia, Myanmar). The platform integrates ride-hailing, food delivery (GrabFood), digital payments (GrabPay/OVO), financial services (GrabFin -- lending, insurance, investments), and advertising into a single ecosystem. Super-App Platform Engineers build the shared infrastructure that enables this service breadth: unified user identity, payment processing, real-time dispatch, merchant platform, and cross-service recommendation systems.

Grab's Singapore-headquartered compensation is competitive within the Southeast Asian tech market and has improved as the company competes with Shopee (Sea Group), Gojek, and international tech companies for engineering talent. The company offers RSU grants (GRAB is NASDAQ-listed), performance bonuses, and competitive Singapore benefits (including housing support). Grab also has US engineering presence in Seattle, where compensation is benchmarked against US tech companies.

The super-app platform role is uniquely complex: engineers must build systems that work across countries with different regulations, payment methods, currencies, and infrastructure quality. This multi-market platform engineering challenge is rare globally and creates highly transferable expertise.

**Level Mapping:** Super-App Platform Engineer at Grab (L5-L7) = L4-L5 at Google, IC4-IC5 at Meta, SDE II-Senior at Amazon, equivalent Uber/DoorDash senior roles

**The Multi-Market Platform Premium**

Grab operates across eight countries with vastly different regulatory environments, payment ecosystems, and consumer behaviors. Platform engineers who can build systems that abstract these differences while maintaining local relevance possess expertise that is extraordinarily valuable. The super-app model (combining rides, food, payments, and financial services) requires architectural decisions that few companies face -- engineers must balance the competing needs of multiple service lines while maintaining platform reliability and performance.

Grab's fintech ambitions (digital banking licenses in Singapore, Malaysia, and Indonesia) add another layer of platform complexity: financial services require bank-grade reliability, compliance, and security, all integrated into the consumer super-app experience.
