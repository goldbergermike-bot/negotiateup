**Negotiation DNA**

DevOps Engineers at Mastercard own the deployment, reliability, and infrastructure automation for a payment network that processes billions of transactions daily with near-zero tolerance for downtime. When Mastercard's systems go down, commerce stops across 210+ countries — the SLA requirements here make most tech companies' reliability standards look relaxed. This role manages CI/CD pipelines, container orchestration, infrastructure-as-code, and observability systems for a platform generating $28B+ in annual revenue. Every deployment must be zero-downtime, every configuration change must be auditable, and every infrastructure component must meet PCI-DSS and SOC 2 compliance requirements.

The current transformation makes DevOps Engineers even more critical. The Agent Pay Acceptance Framework introduces new microservices with unique latency and throughput requirements — autonomous agents expect sub-100ms response times and will retry aggressively, creating traffic patterns that traditional capacity planning doesn't account for. Digital Identity Wallets add biometric authentication services that require specialized GPU infrastructure and real-time inference pipelines. The Trust Orchestrator platform needs a unified deployment and observability layer that spans payment processing, identity verification, and authentication services. DevOps Engineers who can manage this operational complexity while maintaining Mastercard's legendary reliability are in extremely high demand.
