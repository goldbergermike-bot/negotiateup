**Negotiation DNA**

Technical Program Managers at Mastercard orchestrate the delivery of platform initiatives that span dozens of engineering teams, multiple geographies, and regulatory jurisdictions across 210+ countries. This is not lightweight project management — Mastercard's TPMs own the delivery plan, risk management, and cross-functional coordination for programs that directly impact $28B+ in annual revenue. Every program must account for PCI-DSS compliance timelines, network certification windows, issuer and acquirer readiness, and the operational dependencies of a payment network serving 3.3 billion cards.

The current strategic agenda makes TPMs even more critical. The Agent Pay Acceptance Framework is a multi-quarter program requiring coordination across authorization systems, fraud detection, merchant services, and compliance teams — all working on compressed timelines because the competitive landscape for autonomous agent commerce is moving fast. Digital Identity Wallets require TPMs who can manage parallel workstreams across biometric authentication, credential management, government ID integration, and regulatory compliance across different markets. The Trust Orchestrator platform initiative sits on top of both, requiring a TPM who can see the full dependency graph and manage the critical path across all three programs simultaneously. TPMs with this combination of payment network domain expertise and modern platform delivery experience are in short supply.
