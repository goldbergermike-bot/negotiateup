As a Software Engineer at Coupa, you are building the foundational infrastructure that manages over $6 trillion in cumulative spend across 3,000+ enterprise customers including DHL, Uber, and Salesforce. The PE-backed reset under Thoma Bravo has created a rare window where early option grants could see significant appreciation ahead of a future liquidity event — making equity negotiation critical at the point of hire.

Coupa's San Mateo hub serves as the engineering nerve center, and the company's Supply-Chain Resilience positioning means your code directly impacts how Fortune 500 companies manage geopolitical supply disruptions, tariff exposure, and vendor diversification. This strategic importance gives you meaningful leverage in compensation discussions.

The current PE ownership structure means compensation is weighted toward options rather than RSUs. This is both a risk and an opportunity: Thoma Bravo's track record of 3-5x returns on enterprise SaaS investments means your option grants could be worth multiples of their current strike price at a future IPO or secondary sale. Negotiate your option pool aggressively — early employees in PE-backed enterprise platforms rarely get a second bite.
