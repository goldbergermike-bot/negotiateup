**Negotiation DNA**

DevOps Engineers at Coupa are responsible for the infrastructure that underpins a Business Spend Management platform processing over $6 trillion in cumulative enterprise spend. Since Thoma Bravo acquired Coupa for $8 billion in 2023, the company has prioritized infrastructure efficiency and platform reliability as critical drivers of margin expansion — a core PE playbook metric. As a DevOps Engineer, you are building and maintaining the CI/CD pipelines, cloud infrastructure, and deployment automation that enable 3,000+ enterprise customers to rely on Coupa for mission-critical procurement operations.

The Autonomous Sourcing initiative has intensified the demands on DevOps. Autonomous procurement systems must operate with near-zero downtime — when an AI-driven system is routing billions in spend decisions, infrastructure failures have direct financial consequences for enterprises like DHL, Uber, and Salesforce. Coupa's Supply-Chain Resilience positioning also means the platform itself must be resilient: multi-region deployment, failover architectures, and disaster recovery capabilities that match the resilience promise the product makes to its customers.

From Coupa's San Mateo hub, DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure that spans multiple cloud providers and geographies. The AI-powered community intelligence platform adds infrastructure complexity — real-time data aggregation from thousands of enterprise tenants, ML model serving at scale, and data pipeline reliability. Under Thoma Bravo ownership, infrastructure cost optimization is a constant focus, meaning DevOps Engineers who can deliver reliability improvements while reducing cloud spend are exceptionally valuable. Your option grants are priced against the PE valuation, and infrastructure efficiency directly contributes to the margin expansion that drives exit multiples.
