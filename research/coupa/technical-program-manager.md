**Negotiation DNA**

Technical Program Managers at Coupa orchestrate the cross-functional delivery of the company's most complex initiatives. Since Thoma Bravo acquired Coupa for $8 billion in 2023, the TPM role has become indispensable — PE-backed companies operate with compressed timelines and zero tolerance for execution delays, and TPMs are the connective tissue that ensures engineering, product, design, and data science teams deliver cohesively. At Coupa, you are managing programs that span the entire Business Spend Management platform, serving 3,000+ enterprise customers managing over $6 trillion in cumulative spend.

The Autonomous Sourcing initiative is the largest cross-functional program at Coupa, requiring coordination across ML/AI teams, platform engineering, product management, and customer-facing organizations. As a TPM, you own the execution cadence, dependency management, and delivery milestones for this strategic bet. The Supply-Chain Resilience positioning adds complexity — features must be designed, built, tested, and shipped to meet enterprise compliance timelines, geopolitical response windows, and competitive market pressures. This is not traditional project management; it is orchestrating the technical transformation of a platform that enterprises depend on for critical supply chain decisions.

Operating from Coupa's San Mateo hub, TPMs work at the intersection of all technical teams. Under Thoma Bravo ownership, program execution discipline is paramount — every delayed milestone impacts the company's path to exit. The AI-powered community intelligence platform requires TPMs who can manage programs with ML-specific complexity: model training cycles, data pipeline dependencies, A/B testing frameworks, and phased rollouts to enterprise customers. Your option grants should reflect the outsized impact that strong program execution has on the company's ability to hit the revenue and product milestones that drive PE exit value.
