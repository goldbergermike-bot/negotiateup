**Negotiation DNA**

Data Engineers at Deutsche Bank build the data infrastructure that supports one of Europe's largest banks across investment banking, corporate banking, private banking, and asset management. DB's data engineering challenges include cross-border data governance (GDPR, BDSG), multi-jurisdictional regulatory reporting (ECB, BaFin, Fed, FCA), and data integration across legacy systems spanning decades. The bank's technology transformation includes significant investment in modernizing data platforms.

Data Engineers hold VP titles with compensation as base plus discretionary bonus (15-25%) plus deferred compensation. NYC-based roles compete with US bank and tech company packages; London and Frankfurt roles are compensated at competitive European rates. DB has been increasing data engineering compensation as regulatory requirements around data quality and governance intensify.

Competition comes from other European banks (UBS, HSBC, Barclays), US banks, and tech companies. DB's European footprint provides unique data engineering experience that is valued by organizations needing GDPR and European regulatory expertise.

**Level Mapping:** Data Engineer at DB (VP) = L4-L5 at Google, E4-E5 at Meta, SDE II-Senior at Amazon, VP at BofA/JPMorgan, Senior DE at Capital One

**The European Regulatory Data Premium**

DB's data engineering involves navigating European data regulations (GDPR, ECB reporting requirements, BaFin mandates) that are among the strictest in the world. Data engineers who can build compliant, auditable data platforms in this environment develop expertise that is highly valued across European and global financial services. DB's NYC hub provides the opportunity to gain this European regulatory expertise while earning US-competitive compensation.
