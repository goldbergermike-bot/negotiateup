**Negotiation DNA**

DevOps Engineers at Visa operate at the infrastructure layer of the world's most critical payment network — a system that must maintain five-nines uptime while processing 65,000+ transactions per second across 200+ countries. This is not startup DevOps where a deployment failure means a broken landing page; at Visa, a misconfigured pipeline or a failed rollback can halt commerce for millions of people and cost tens of millions of dollars per minute. The January 2026 launch of the Trusted Agent Protocol adds an entirely new deployment surface: AI-agent transaction services, credential management infrastructure, and trust-verification pipelines that must be rolled out globally without disrupting the existing VisaNet. DevOps Engineers on this initiative are Standard-Bearers ensuring that the future of AI commerce ships reliably.

Visa's DevOps compensation reflects the operational criticality of the role. Base salaries are competitive with Big Tech SRE/DevOps roles, and RSU grants ($120K-$220K over four years) serve as a retention mechanism for engineers whose deep knowledge of Visa's deployment infrastructure makes them effectively irreplaceable. The bonus component (12-18% of base) is tied to reliability metrics — deployment frequency, mean time to recovery, change failure rate — all of which will be scrutinized intensely during the Protocol rollout. Visa's $35B+ revenue and NYSE: V stock performance make the equity component a strong wealth-building lever. Candidates with experience in zero-downtime deployments, multi-region infrastructure, and financial-grade CI/CD systems will command the upper quartile of the band.
