**Negotiation DNA**

Product Managers at Visa do not ship features — they shape the economics of global commerce. Every product decision cascades across a network that processes $14+ trillion annually, serving 4B+ cardholders through partnerships with thousands of financial institutions in 200+ countries. The January 2026 launch of the Trusted Agent Protocol is the most consequential product initiative at Visa since the introduction of tokenization: it establishes the rules by which AI agents will transact on behalf of humans. Product Managers on this initiative are Standard-Bearers defining the trust contracts, risk parameters, and monetization models that will govern the next era of autonomous commerce.

Visa's PM compensation reflects the outsized impact of product decisions on a $35B+ revenue base. Base salaries are competitive with Big Tech, and the RSU component ($150K-$280K over four years) is structured to retain PMs through multi-year product cycles. The bonus component (typically 15-25% of base) is tied to product delivery and adoption metrics — and with the Trusted Agent Protocol, these metrics will be among the most closely watched KPIs in the company. Candidates with experience in platform product management, API design, developer ecosystems, or financial infrastructure will command the upper half of the band. The intersection of AI and payments is a greenfield product domain, and Visa is actively competing with Mastercard, Stripe, and Big Tech for PMs who can navigate it.

### Trusted Agent Protocol — The Standard-Bearer Premium

**Lever 3 — Competitive Intelligence Premium:** "Every major payment network and fintech company is racing to define the AI-commerce trust layer. **Lever 4 — Cross-Functional Influence Scope:** "This PM role requires coordinating across engineering, security, legal, compliance, partnerships, and go-to-market — all simultaneously, across multiple geographies.
