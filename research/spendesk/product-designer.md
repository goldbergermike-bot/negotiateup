**Negotiation DNA**

Product Designers at Spendesk face a design challenge unlike most B2B SaaS roles: making financial compliance invisible. The company's Agentic Integrity strategy and Zero-Human Audit vision mean that autonomous compliance agents must operate seamlessly behind an interface that 5,000+ companies use daily for cards, invoices, expenses, and budgets. Backed by General Atlantic and Index Ventures at a $500M+ valuation, Spendesk needs designers who can translate complex regulatory requirements into intuitive workflows — making the 99.8% catch-rate feel effortless to the end user.

The Compliance Sovereignty positioning adds a unique dimension to the design role. European companies choosing Spendesk are choosing a platform that handles spend under EU regulatory frameworks, and the design must communicate trust, sovereignty, and control at every interaction. This is not cosmetic design — it is trust architecture. Every design decision either reinforces or undermines the perception that Spendesk is the most compliant, most trustworthy spend management platform in Europe.

The all-in-one platform surface — spanning cards, invoices, expenses, and budgets — means Product Designers work across multiple product areas with shared design systems and interaction patterns. Consistency at this scale requires design systems thinking, not just screen-level craft. Equity is offered through options (BSPCE for French employees) with 4-year vesting, and designers should negotiate for packages that reflect the strategic importance of design in a compliance-first product.

### Zero-Human Audit — The Compliance Sovereignty Premium

**Lever 1 — Agentic Integrity UX Design:** "Spendesk's Agentic Integrity vision means autonomous compliance agents will be operating behind the interfaces I design. Making Zero-Human Audit invisible to users — while still providing transparency and trust — is a design challenge that requires deep understanding of both compliance workflows and user psychology. I'd like a base of €72,000 to reflect this specialized design challenge that goes beyond typical B2B SaaS design work."

**Lever 2 — 99.8% Catch-Rate Trust Design:** "The 99.8% catch-rate is meaningless if users don't trust it. Designing interfaces that communicate compliance confidence — showing users that their spend is being monitored, validated, and audited without creating anxiety or friction — is what separates Spendesk from competitors. I'm designing trust, not just screens. I'd like my options to reflect this with a grant of €35,000 over four years."

**Lever 3 — Compliance Sovereignty Visual Language:** "Compliance Sovereignty needs a design language that communicates European data sovereignty, regulatory independence, and institutional-grade security — all without making the product feel bureaucratic. This is brand-critical design work that directly impacts how CFOs and finance teams evaluate Spendesk against US-based alternatives. I'm asking for a 12% annual bonus target tied to product adoption metrics."

**Lever 4 — All-in-One Design Systems Premium:** "Designing a cohesive experience across cards, invoices, expenses, and budgets — each with different user workflows, regulatory requirements, and data density — requires design systems thinking at a level that most B2B SaaS companies don't need. I'd like a €6,000 signing bonus to offset the ramp-up time required to understand the full platform surface and the EU compliance context."
