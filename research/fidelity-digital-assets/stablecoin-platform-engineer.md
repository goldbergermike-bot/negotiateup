#### Negotiation DNA

The Stablecoin Platform Engineer is the **signature engineering role** at Fidelity Digital Assets — the most specialized, most impactful, and highest-compensated IC position in the organization. This role exists because of one product: the Fidelity Digital Dollar (FIDD). Fidelity Digital Assets, the digital asset arm of Fidelity Investments ($14T+ AUM parent), created the Stablecoin Platform Engineer role specifically to attract the small number of engineers globally who possess the end-to-end expertise required to architect, build, and operate an institutional-grade stablecoin platform from the protocol layer through the compliance layer. Candidates are expected to bring 8-15 years of experience with demonstrated mastery of smart contract engineering, cryptographic protocol design, financial infrastructure systems, and regulatory compliance architecture. The +25-35% Stablecoin Platform Premium is the highest premium applied to any role at Fidelity Digital Assets, reflecting the near-nonexistent talent pool at this intersection of skills. There are estimated to be fewer than 100 engineers globally who possess the full stack of competencies this role requires — smart contract security, institutional settlement systems, reserve attestation protocols, multi-chain interoperability, and regulatory compliance architecture. Compensation includes Fidelity Profit-Based RSUs vesting over four years, tied to the parent company's enterprise value. The Stablecoin Platform Engineer is the ultimate architect of institutional-grade stablecoins — the single engineer most responsible for FIDD's technical integrity, scalability, and regulatory compliance.

#### Fidelity Digital Dollar — The Institutional Stablecoin Architect

In February 2026, Fidelity launched the **Fidelity Digital Dollar (FIDD)** — a regulated, institutional-grade stablecoin fully backed by US Treasury securities. FIDD is the centerpiece of Fidelity's digital asset strategy and the single most important product in the Fidelity Digital Assets portfolio.

As a Stablecoin Platform Engineer, you **are** the FIDD platform:

- **Protocol Architecture:** Defining the end-to-end protocol architecture of FIDD — from the smart contract layer (mint/burn mechanics, access controls, upgrade patterns) through the settlement layer (multi-chain deployment, cross-chain bridging, atomic settlement) to the compliance layer (on-chain KYC/AML enforcement, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring). Your architecture decisions govern how billions of dollars flow through Fidelity's on-chain infrastructure.
- **Smart Contract Engineering:** Writing, auditing, and hardening the core FIDD smart contracts that control token issuance, redemption, and transfer. These contracts are the single highest-risk code in Fidelity's entire technology stack — a vulnerability here could result in catastrophic loss. You own the security of the most critical codebase at the world's largest asset manager.
- **Reserve Attestation Protocol:** Designing and implementing the cryptographic proof-of-reserve system that continuously verifies FIDD's 1:1 backing by US Treasury securities. This system is the trust backbone of the entire FIDD platform — it must be cryptographically sound, tamper-proof, and independently verifiable.
- **Multi-Chain Interoperability:** Architecting the cross-chain infrastructure that enables FIDD to operate across Ethereum, Solana, and Fidelity's proprietary settlement layer. You design the bridging protocols, chain-abstraction layers, and settlement finality mechanisms that make FIDD a universal institutional settlement token.
- **Institutional Settlement Engine:** Building the high-throughput, low-latency settlement engine that processes institutional FIDD transactions — large-block mints, institutional redemptions, programmatic treasury operations — with full audit trails and regulatory compliance baked into every transaction.
- **Regulatory Protocol Design:** Embedding regulatory compliance directly into the FIDD protocol — designing on-chain mechanisms for KYC/AML enforcement, sanctions list updates, transaction reporting, and regulatory holds that meet the requirements of every jurisdiction where FIDD operates.

Key negotiation framing:

- **"I am the engineer most responsible for the technical integrity of Fidelity's stablecoin — the single product that bridges the world's largest asset manager to on-chain finance."** This is not a typical engineering role. This is the defining technical position of Fidelity's digital asset strategy.
- **Extreme talent scarcity:** Fewer than 100 engineers globally possess the full stack of competencies this role requires. The +25-35% Premium is not generous — it is the minimum required to attract candidates from this talent pool, where competing offers from protocol foundations, L1 chains, and well-funded crypto startups routinely exceed $500K TC.
- **Asymmetric risk/reward:** The Stablecoin Platform Engineer is personally responsible for code that controls billions of dollars. This asymmetric risk profile — where a single bug can cause catastrophic financial loss — justifies compensation that reflects the magnitude of assets under protection.
- **Revenue cornerstone:** FIDD has the potential to become Fidelity's largest digital asset revenue line, generating hundreds of millions annually from settlement fees, reserve yield, and institutional adoption. The Stablecoin Platform Engineer is the individual most directly responsible for the technical foundation of this revenue.
