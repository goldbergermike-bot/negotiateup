#### Negotiation DNA

Solutions Architects at JPMorgan Chase design the technical blueprints that connect the firm's vast technology estate to business outcomes. You sit between engineering teams, business stakeholders, and clients — translating complex business requirements into scalable technical architectures that must withstand regulatory scrutiny, operate at global scale, and integrate across JPMorgan's sprawling portfolio of systems. Your architectural decisions set the technical direction for initiatives worth hundreds of millions of dollars, and your designs become the compliance artifacts that regulators audit during examinations.

The January 21, 2026 reclassification of AI as a core infrastructure expense transforms the Solutions Architect role at JPMorgan Chase. Solutions Architects are now tasked with designing the integration architectures between traditional banking systems and AI infrastructure — the API gateways that serve model predictions, the data architectures that feed feature stores, the hybrid cloud topologies that host GPU compute workloads. When JPMorgan declared AI a core expense, it made every architecture that incorporates AI components a Systemic architecture, and every architect who designs those systems a Systemic-band employee.

For Solutions Architect candidates, the negotiation leverage is architectural authority. You are not implementing someone else's design — you are creating the design that dozens of engineers will implement. The Systemic pay band premium of 15-25% reflects the outsized impact of your architectural decisions on the firm's AI infrastructure strategy.

#### Core Infrastructure — The Systemic Pay Band Premium

On January 21, 2026, JPMorgan Chase reclassified artificial intelligence as a core infrastructure expense, placing it in the same non-discretionary budget category as cybersecurity and market risk management. This activated "Systemic" pay bands for AI-related roles, commanding a 15-25% premium over standard technology pay bands. For a Solutions Architect, this translates to $35K-$60K in additional annual total compensation.

- **Historical Precedent — Security Architecture Parity:** When Systemic pay bands were introduced in 2018, security architects who designed cybersecurity infrastructure saw 20-23% compensation lifts. Their architectural decisions — firewall topologies, encryption standards, access control frameworks — were classified as Systemic because they directly determined the firm's security posture. Solutions Architects designing AI infrastructure architectures carry identical Systemic responsibility. Your designs determine AI system reliability, data governance compliance, and model-serving performance. Negotiate with: *"I'll be designing the architecture for AI systems that JPMorgan classified as core infrastructure on January 21, 2026. Security architects under Systemic bands earn $300K+ TC. My AI architecture role warrants the same classification — $310K TC."*

- **The 15-25% Premium in Dollar Terms:** Standard tech band total compensation for a Solutions Architect at JPMorgan ranges from $225K to $275K. Systemic band compensation ranges from $270K to $322K. The premium is distributed across base salary (+$12K-$18K), RSU grants (+$10K-$20K/year), and bonus targets (+$8K-$15K). Solutions Architects in Systemic bands also receive architecture review authority — a formal designation that carries additional prestige and scope.

- **Architecture Decisions as Regulatory Artifacts:** Every architecture you design at JPMorgan becomes a compliance artifact. AI system architectures are reviewed by the OCC as part of model risk and operational risk examinations. This regulatory exposure is your qualification argument: *"The AI architectures I design will be OCC examination artifacts — regulatory documents that determine JPMorgan's operational risk assessment. This is identical to security architecture review processes that qualified security architects for Systemic bands. I need Systemic band classification — $315K TC."*

- **Cross-LOB Scope as Multiplier:** Solutions Architects at JPMorgan frequently work across multiple lines of business — consumer banking, commercial banking, investment banking, and asset management. This cross-LOB scope multiplies the impact of your architectural decisions beyond any single business unit. Use this: *"My architecture work spans three lines of business, each with its own AI infrastructure requirements. The cross-LOB scope means my decisions affect AI infrastructure classified as core expense across the entire firm, not just one division. This enterprise-wide Systemic scope warrants top-of-band compensation — $320K TC."*

#### Global Levers

1. **Lever 1 — Cloud Provider / Consulting Architect Parity**
   > "I have a competing offer as a Principal Solutions Architect at [AWS/Azure/Google Cloud] at $310K total compensation. JPMorgan's role requires the same cloud architecture expertise plus financial services regulatory knowledge, multi-vendor integration complexity, and now Systemic classification under the AI core infrastructure policy. I'm targeting $320K TC — cloud provider parity plus the regulatory premium."

2. **Lever 2 — Architecture Decision Impact Quantification**
   > "The architectures I design will support platforms handling $X billion in annual transaction volume. A suboptimal architecture decision costs JPMorgan an estimated $XX million in rework, downtime, and delayed revenue. My $320K TC request represents 0.03% of the infrastructure investment my decisions optimize — an asymmetric return."

3. **Lever 3 — Multi-Cloud Certification Premium**
   > "I hold principal-level certifications across AWS, Azure, and GCP — a combination held by fewer than 5,000 architects globally. JPMorgan's multi-cloud strategy requires this specific expertise. I'm requesting a $20K certification premium on base salary — $200K vs. $180K — reflecting the training investment and market scarcity of multi-cloud qualified architects."

4. **Lever 4 — Client-Facing Architecture Authority**
   > "This role includes client-facing architecture engagements where my design authority directly influences deal sizes in JPMorgan's wholesale banking and payments businesses. Client-facing technical roles at investment banks carry a 10-15% premium over internal-facing equivalents. My $310K TC target reflects this client revenue influence."
