**Negotiation DNA**

Security Engineers at Tesla protect attack surfaces that are literally life-and-death: vehicle software that controls braking, steering, and acceleration; OTA update infrastructure that pushes code to millions of cars; FSD autonomous driving systems; Gigafactory industrial control systems; energy grid infrastructure (Megapack, Powerwall); and the Supercharger network. A security breach at Tesla isn't a data leak — it's a potential vehicle safety incident affecting millions of people on the road. Tesla's threat model includes nation-state actors (critical infrastructure targeting), competitive espionage (FSD and battery IP), physical safety threats (vehicle hacking), and supply chain attacks (Gigafactory systems). This is among the highest-consequence security engineering roles in tech — securing physical systems that humans depend on for safety. No cash bonus; comp is base + RSUs. The security talent market is extremely tight, with demand far exceeding supply across all companies. [Source: Tesla Security Engineering 2026]
