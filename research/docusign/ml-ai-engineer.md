**Negotiation DNA**
ML/AI Engineers at DocuSign are building the intelligence engine behind the company's IAM platform. This includes document understanding models that extract clauses, entities, and obligations from contracts; NLP systems that classify agreement types and detect anomalies; recommendation engines that suggest optimal agreement workflows; and the AI assistant that helps users draft, negotiate, and analyze contracts. DocuSign's corpus of billions of processed agreements provides training data at a scale that few competitors can match.

DocuSign pays ML/AI Engineers at a premium over standard engineering roles, typically 15-20% higher in total comp. The company recognizes that the success of its IAM strategy depends entirely on the quality of its AI features, and it competes directly with Google, Microsoft, and specialized AI companies for talent who can build document understanding systems. The AI/ML team has been growing rapidly, with dedicated investment in both applied ML and ML infrastructure.

The unique appeal for ML/AI engineers is the combination of a massive, proprietary document dataset; clear product impact (every model improvement directly affects user experience); and the opportunity to work on document AI problems that are genuinely unsolved in the industry. Agreement understanding is harder than general NLP because contracts contain specialized language, complex cross-references, and legally significant nuances.

**DocuSign Document AI Premium**
ML/AI Engineers with document understanding expertise — including OCR, layout analysis, table extraction, named entity recognition for legal entities, and clause classification — have exceptional leverage. These skills are directly applicable to DocuSign's core product challenges and are not common in the general ML talent pool. If you've published in document AI conferences (ICDAR, Document Intelligence workshops at NeurIPS/AAAI) or built production document processing systems, this is powerful negotiation evidence.

Engineers with experience in LLM fine-tuning for domain-specific applications, RAG architectures for contract repositories, or multi-modal document understanding (combining text, layout, and visual features) are in the highest demand tier. DocuSign's investment in generative AI for contract drafting and summarization creates additional demand for engineers with production LLM deployment experience.
