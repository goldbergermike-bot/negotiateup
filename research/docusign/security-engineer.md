**Negotiation DNA**
Security Engineers at DocuSign protect one of the most sensitive document platforms in the world. DocuSign handles legally binding agreements, personal identification documents, financial contracts, and healthcare records. A security breach at DocuSign would not just be a data leak — it could invalidate legal agreements, enable fraud, and destroy the trust that is DocuSign's most valuable brand asset. This existential security risk creates genuine leverage for security professionals who can protect the platform.

DocuSign compensates Security Engineers at a premium over standard engineering roles, typically 10-15% higher in total comp. The company maintains multiple compliance certifications (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, FedRAMP, HIPAA, and regional certifications), and security engineers are responsible for maintaining and extending these certifications as the platform evolves. The IAM expansion increases the security surface area significantly — more data processing, more AI features, more integrations — which means security engineering demand is growing.

DocuSign's security organization covers application security (secure SDLC, code review, pen testing), cloud infrastructure security (AWS security, network security, encryption), identity and access management (digital signatures, authentication, PKI), and compliance engineering. Engineers who bridge multiple security domains are most valued.

**DocuSign Digital Trust Premium**
Security Engineers with expertise in digital signature technology — PKI infrastructure, certificate management, eIDAS compliance, digital signature standards (XAdES, PAdES, CAdES) — occupy an exceptionally specialized niche at DocuSign. Very few security engineers globally have deep expertise in digital signature security, and those who do are critical to DocuSign's core product integrity. If you bring this expertise, your negotiating position is extremely strong.

Additionally, security engineers who understand AI security — protecting ML models from adversarial attacks, ensuring data privacy in AI training pipelines, and building secure AI inference systems — are in growing demand as DocuSign embeds AI across the IAM platform. The intersection of document security and AI security is a novel domain with very few experienced practitioners.
