**Negotiation DNA**
DevOps Engineers at DocuSign maintain the infrastructure behind one of the most mission-critical SaaS platforms in business. When DocuSign goes down, real estate closings halt, procurement workflows stop, and legal teams cannot execute agreements. The platform handles over 1.5 million agreements daily with strict uptime SLA requirements, and DevOps engineers are responsible for the CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, and reliability practices that keep this high-throughput, high-stakes system running.

DocuSign compensates DevOps Engineers competitively within the mid-cap SaaS tier. The role benefits from the criticality of the platform — DocuSign's brand is built on trust and reliability, which means infrastructure engineering is a first-class priority, not a cost center. DevOps engineers have direct access to leadership and meaningful influence over architectural and tooling decisions.

The tech stack includes AWS as the primary cloud provider, Kubernetes for container orchestration, Terraform for infrastructure-as-code, and modern observability tools (Datadog, PagerDuty). DocuSign has been investing in platform engineering to improve developer productivity, creating opportunities for DevOps engineers who want to build internal developer platforms rather than purely operational roles.

**Level Mapping:** DocuSign IC2/IC3 ~ Twilio Senior/Staff SRE | Datadog Senior/Staff | Salesforce MTS/LMTS Platform | Google L4/L5 SRE

**DocuSign Platform Reliability Premium**
DevOps Engineers who bring experience with high-throughput document processing systems, legally compliant data handling (including data residency and encryption requirements), and multi-region deployment architectures have specific leverage at DocuSign. The platform's compliance requirements — SOC 2 Type II, FedRAMP, ISO 27001, and industry-specific certifications — mean infrastructure engineers must balance speed of delivery with strict security and compliance guardrails.

Engineers with experience in zero-downtime deployment strategies for mission-critical SaaS platforms, or who have built sophisticated canary deployment and feature flag systems, are particularly valuable. DocuSign's evolution toward the IAM platform also means infrastructure needs are growing — more AI workloads, more data processing pipelines, more real-time analytics — creating expansion opportunities for DevOps teams.
