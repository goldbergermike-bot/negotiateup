**Negotiation DNA**
DevOps Engineers at Zuora maintain the infrastructure behind a financial transaction processing platform that handles billions of dollars in subscription billing. When Zuora's platform has downtime during billing cycle runs, customers cannot process payments, which directly impacts their revenue. This financial criticality means infrastructure engineers must build systems that are not only highly available but also financially accurate — a billing infrastructure bug that miscalculates charges has legal and financial consequences.

Zuora compensates DevOps Engineers in line with its smaller company profile, below hyperscaler and large SaaS DevOps compensation but competitive for the fintech/billing technology sector. The trade-off is meaningful: at Zuora, DevOps engineers have significant influence over infrastructure decisions, broader scope than at larger companies, and the opportunity to shape the platform's reliability practices.

The tech stack is cloud-native (AWS, Kubernetes, Terraform) with the added complexity of financial-grade reliability and compliance requirements (SOC 2, PCI DSS for payment processing). DevOps engineers at Zuora work at the intersection of standard cloud infrastructure and financial systems engineering.

**Zuora Financial Infrastructure Premium**
DevOps Engineers who bring experience with financial transaction processing infrastructure — payment systems, billing platforms, or any system where financial accuracy and audit trails are critical — have strong leverage. Engineers with experience in compliance-certified infrastructure (SOC 2, PCI DSS, ISO 27001) who can automate compliance controls and evidence collection bring particular value to Zuora's operations.
