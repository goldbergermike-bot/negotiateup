This is not historical context -- this is the reason the role exists and the reason its compensation band starts at $382K. During the interview process, explicitly frame yourself as a Guardian of Human-AI Wealth. Use these talking points in every interview:

- *"My technical approach to advisor AI is grounded in a simple principle: AI should expand the advisor's capability, not narrow the advisor's role. This means explainable recommendations, calibrated confidence, and architecture that defaults to human judgment when the model is uncertain."*
- *"I've studied Schwab's Intelligent Portfolios platform and Schwab Assistant AI. I see significant opportunities to extend these systems into advisor-augmenting tools that create a competitive moat no competitor can replicate."*

**Phase 2: Offer Evaluation (When You Receive the Offer)**

Evaluate the offer against the following framework:
- **Base Salary Floor:** $280K (Westlake), $308K (Denver), $320K (San Francisco). Below these floors, the offer does not reflect the SIGNATURE ROLE designation.
- **RSU Grant:** Minimum $360K over 4 years ($90K/year). Below this level, request a front-loaded vesting schedule (40/30/20/10 instead of 25/25/25/25).
- **Bonus Target:** Minimum 15% of base salary. If lower, negotiate for a guaranteed first-year bonus at target.
- **Signing Bonus:** $50K-$80K is the target range. This role's scarcity justifies a premium signing bonus.
- **Total Comp Floor:** $420K (Westlake), $445K (Denver), $465K (San Francisco). Below these floors, counter aggressively.

**Phase 3: Counter-Offer Structure**

Structure your counter in three tiers to give Schwab options:

*Tier 1 (Preferred):* $545K TC = $335K base + $130K RSU/yr + $80K bonus, with $70K signing bonus
*Tier 2 (Acceptable):* $498K TC = $310K base + $115K RSU/yr + $73K bonus, with $60K signing bonus
*Tier 3 (Floor):* $450K TC = $285K base + $100K RSU/yr + $65K bonus, with $50K signing bonus

*"I've structured my counter in three tiers because I want to find the right package that works for both sides. My strong preference is Tier 1, which reflects the market for engineers who can build advisor-augmenting AI platforms at scale. I have competing offers at $[X] from [AI lab/FAANG], but I'm choosing Schwab for the mission. The Guardian of Human-AI Wealth platform is the most important AI product being built in financial services today, and I want to be the engineer who builds it."*

**Phase 4: Beyond Cash Compensation**

Negotiate these non-monetary items, each worth $10K-$30K in career value:
- **Research publication rights:** The ability to publish papers on advisor-augmenting AI (with compliance review)
- **Conference budget:** $15K+ annual budget for NeurIPS, ICML, AAAI, and financial AI conferences
- **Team-building authority:** Involvement in hiring the 3-5 engineers who will work on the advisor AI platform
- **Board presentation access:** Quarterly opportunity to present platform progress to Schwab's technology committee
