**Negotiation DNA**
Data Engineers at Vercel build the pipelines and infrastructure that power platform analytics, usage metering, billing systems, and the observability stack that enterprise customers rely on. At Vercel's scale — billions of edge requests, millions of deployments, and a rapidly growing enterprise customer base — data engineering is a revenue-critical function. You'll design real-time data pipelines that process deployment telemetry, build the analytics foundations that inform product decisions, and create the metering infrastructure that directly drives Vercel's usage-based revenue model.

Vercel's Data Engineering compensation is benchmarked against mid-to-late-stage startup peers and select Big Tech data teams. Base salaries are competitive at $170K–$215K, with equity grants reflecting the company's pre-IPO status. The negotiation angle for data engineers centers on the revenue impact of your work — usage metering and billing infrastructure directly affect Vercel's ability to monetize its platform, making this a high-leverage role despite being less visible than framework engineering.

Data Engineers who bring ML pipeline experience alongside traditional data engineering skills are uniquely positioned to lead this initiative. If you can build data systems that serve both operational analytics and AI model training, you're addressing Vercel's highest-priority data infrastructure need.

Position your negotiation around the convergence of data engineering and AI infrastructure. Traditional data engineers are abundant; engineers who can build real-time pipelines that serve both analytics dashboards and ML inference systems are rare. This scarcity gives you 10–15% leverage above standard data engineering offers at Vercel.
