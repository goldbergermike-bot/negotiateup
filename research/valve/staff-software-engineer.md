**Negotiation DNA**

Staff-level engineers at Valve (though the company has no formal titles) are the technical leaders who define Valve's most impactful work. In Valve's flat org, "Staff-level" means you're the engineer others seek out for architecture guidance, the person who identifies and drives the most important technical initiatives, and someone whose peer evaluation scores consistently place them in the top tier of profit-sharing allocation. At this level, profit-sharing becomes truly exceptional — reports from Valve employees suggest top-tier engineers receive $200K-$400K+ annually in profit-sharing, pushing total comp to $500K-$600K+ or beyond. Staff-level engineers at Valve work on the most technically challenging problems: Steam's recommendation engine, Source 2 engine architecture, Steam Deck hardware/software integration, and VR technology. The total comp at this level rivals or exceeds FAANG principal-level roles, making Valve one of the highest-paying employers in the technology industry for top performers.
