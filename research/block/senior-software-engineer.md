**Negotiation DNA**

The Senior Software Engineer is the workhorse of Block's Agentic Foundation strategy. When Block launched the Agentic AI Foundation in December 2025, it bet the company's future on autonomous agents that could independently handle merchant onboarding for Square, automate Cash App financial advisory flows, and orchestrate Bitcoin transactions across TBD's decentralized protocols. The February 2026 workforce reduction of 10% made this bet explicit: Block is not hiring volume — it is assembling a Lean Elite of senior-level builders who can own entire agent subsystems end-to-end.

At the L5/Senior level, Block expects engineers who can architect, build, and ship autonomous agent capabilities without heavy managerial oversight. This is the level where the Agentic Foundation's core modules are being written — the inference pipelines, the tool-use orchestration layers, the safety guardrails for financial agents operating on real money. Block's $22B+ revenue runs through Square and Cash App, and autonomous agents touching that revenue stream demand engineers with deep expertise in distributed systems, ML serving infrastructure, and financial compliance.

The post-restructuring reality creates a significant negotiation advantage. Block eliminated mid-level generalist positions but is aggressively backfilling with senior engineers who can accelerate the agentic roadmap. Competing offers from Google, Meta, Stripe, and Coinbase are common at this level, and Block's recruiting team has budget flexibility to close candidates who can credibly contribute to the Agentic Foundation. The Bitcoin treasury strategy (Block holds over $1B in BTC) also means RSU grants carry additional upside exposure to crypto-correlated equity appreciation.
