**Negotiation DNA**

Engineering Managers at Block in 2026 operate in one of the most challenging and high-leverage leadership environments in fintech. The December 2025 launch of the Agentic AI Foundation transformed Block's engineering organization: teams are now building autonomous agents that handle Square merchant payments, Cash App consumer transactions, and TBD Bitcoin operations with minimal human intervention. The February 2026 workforce reduction of 10% eliminated management layers and broadened spans of control, creating the Lean Elite leadership model where every EM owns a larger, more technically demanding portfolio.

The post-restructuring Block EM role is fundamentally different from pre-2026 engineering management at the company. Managers now oversee teams building autonomous agent systems — not traditional CRUD applications or incremental feature work. This requires EMs who can recruit and retain elite engineers, make technical tradeoffs in agent architecture, and maintain velocity on a compressed timeline. Block's $22B+ revenue flows through Square and Cash App, and the agents these teams build will directly impact transaction throughput, fraud detection, and customer experience.

Block's Bitcoin treasury strategy and its cross-ecosystem ambitions (Square, Cash App, TIDAL, TBD) mean EMs must coordinate across business units with different technical stacks and regulatory environments. The Lean Elite model demands managers who are player-coaches — capable of reading code, challenging architectural decisions, and shipping alongside their teams. This elevated expectation creates significant negotiation leverage for candidates who combine technical depth with proven leadership in high-velocity, high-stakes environments.
