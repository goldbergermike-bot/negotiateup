**Negotiation DNA**

Bridgewater Associates, the world's largest hedge fund with approximately $150 billion under management, depends on Data Engineers to build and maintain the data infrastructure that feeds its systematic investment strategies. Data is the foundational asset at Bridgewater — every investment thesis, risk model, and portfolio construction decision in the Pure Alpha, All Weather, and Optimal Portfolio strategies begins with data. As a Data Engineer, you will design and operate pipelines that ingest, transform, and serve massive volumes of financial market data, alternative data, and proprietary research data to the firm's investment teams and automated systems. Base salaries range from $160K to $220K, with discretionary annual bonuses of 50% to 100%+ of base, producing total compensation of $240K to $440K+.

Bridgewater is a private partnership with no public equity grants. All variable compensation is delivered through discretionary annual bonuses evaluated under the firm's Principles-based management framework. The "radical transparency" culture means your performance is continuously assessed through tools like the Dot Collector, and your contributions to data reliability, pipeline performance, and data quality are quantified and visible to leadership. This transparency can work in your favor — measurable improvements to data infrastructure are directly attributable and can strengthen your case during annual compensation reviews.

Data Engineers at Bridgewater are recruited from both the quantitative finance and big tech talent pools. The firm competes with D.E. Shaw, Two Sigma, Citadel, and Renaissance Technologies for candidates with financial data experience, and with Google, Meta, Amazon, and Databricks for candidates with large-scale data infrastructure expertise. The all-cash compensation model means Bridgewater offers higher base salaries and bonus guarantees compared to equity-heavy tech packages to remain competitive.

**Level Mapping:** Data Engineer at Bridgewater = L4 Data Engineer at Google, E4 Data Engineer at Meta, SDE II (Data) at Amazon

**The Hedge Fund Premium**

Data Engineers at Bridgewater earn a premium because the quality and reliability of data infrastructure directly determines the quality of investment decisions managing $150 billion. A data pipeline failure or data quality issue can result in missed trading signals or erroneous risk assessments with real dollar consequences. The firm's systematic approach means virtually every investment decision flows through engineered data systems, making Data Engineers essential — not support staff. This direct investment impact, combined with competition from both quant funds and tech companies, drives compensation well above typical industry levels.
