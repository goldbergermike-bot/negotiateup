**Negotiation DNA**

Bridgewater Associates, the world's largest hedge fund managing approximately $150 billion in assets, hires Data Scientists to develop quantitative models, statistical analyses, and research frameworks that inform its systematic investment strategies. At Bridgewater, Data Scientists work at the frontier of financial research — building predictive models for macroeconomic indicators, analyzing alternative data sources for investment signals, and stress-testing portfolio strategies across historical and simulated market conditions. The role sits closer to the investment process than at most firms, meaning your work has a direct and measurable impact on how billions of dollars are allocated across the Pure Alpha, All Weather, and Optimal Portfolio strategies. Base salaries range from $170K to $240K, with discretionary annual bonuses of 60% to 120%+ of base, yielding total compensation of $272K to $528K+.

As a private partnership, Bridgewater offers no public equity, RSUs, or stock options. All variable compensation is through discretionary annual bonuses determined by individual performance, team contribution, and fund profitability. The firm's radical transparency culture and Principles-based management mean that your research contributions are evaluated with exceptional rigor — the Dot Collector feedback system and Baseball Card performance profiles provide granular, real-time assessments of your work. Data Scientists who produce research that generates alpha or improves risk management see their bonus multiples increase significantly over time.

Bridgewater competes for Data Scientists against other elite quant funds (D.E. Shaw, Two Sigma, Citadel, Renaissance Technologies) and top tech companies with strong data science cultures (Google, Meta, Netflix, Apple). Candidates with backgrounds in econometrics, applied mathematics, physics, or computational finance are particularly valued. The firm's willingness to pay premium compensation for quantitative talent with financial market knowledge creates strong negotiating leverage for well-qualified candidates.

**Level Mapping:** Data Scientist at Bridgewater = L4/L5 Data Scientist at Google, IC4/IC5 Research Scientist at Meta, Applied Scientist II at Amazon

**The Hedge Fund Premium**

Data Scientists at Bridgewater command premium compensation because their models and research directly influence investment decisions across $150 billion in assets. A single predictive signal or risk factor identified by a Data Scientist can generate or protect hundreds of millions in returns. Unlike data science roles at tech companies where the connection to revenue can be indirect, at Bridgewater the link between quantitative research and fund performance is immediate and quantifiable. This direct alpha contribution, combined with the scarcity of candidates who can blend statistical rigor with macroeconomic and financial market knowledge, drives compensation well above tech industry norms for comparable roles.
