**Negotiation DNA**

Bridgewater Associates, the world's largest hedge fund with approximately $150 billion under management, hires Security Engineers to protect the firm's investment strategies, trading systems, proprietary research, and sensitive client data from cyber threats. Security at Bridgewater is not an afterthought — the firm's systematic investment strategies represent some of the most valuable intellectual property in finance, and a security breach could compromise trading signals worth billions of dollars. Security Engineers at the firm work across application security, network security, cloud security, identity and access management, threat detection and response, and security architecture for the firm's investment technology infrastructure. Base salaries range from $165K to $235K, with discretionary annual bonuses of 40% to 80%+ of base, yielding total compensation of $231K to $423K+.

Bridgewater is a private partnership with no public equity or RSU grants. Variable compensation comes entirely through discretionary annual bonuses evaluated under the firm's Principles-based management and radical transparency framework. Security Engineers are assessed on threat prevention effectiveness, incident response quality, security architecture improvements, and their ability to balance security controls with the velocity needs of the engineering organization. The Dot Collector system ensures that your security recommendations and trade-off decisions are documented and visible.

The hedge fund industry is an increasingly high-value target for nation-state actors, cybercriminal organizations, and insider threats. Bridgewater competes for security talent against other financial institutions (D.E. Shaw, Two Sigma, Citadel, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan), top tech companies (Google, Amazon, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks), and government/intelligence agencies. Security engineers with experience in financial services, critical infrastructure, or high-threat environments command significant negotiating leverage.

**Level Mapping:** Security Engineer at Bridgewater = L4/L5 Security Engineer at Google, E4/E5 Security Engineer at Meta, Security Engineer II/Senior at Amazon

**The Hedge Fund Premium**

Security Engineers at Bridgewater earn a premium because the consequences of a security failure are catastrophic — exposure of proprietary trading strategies, unauthorized access to trading systems, or a data breach affecting institutional investors could threaten the firm's existence. The firm protects $150 billion in assets and intellectual property that represents decades of systematic investment research. This existential risk profile, combined with increasing regulatory requirements (SEC cybersecurity rules) and the sophistication of threats targeting hedge funds, means Bridgewater must attract top-tier security talent and compensate them accordingly. The all-cash structure means security engineers receive highly competitive base and bonus packages without the uncertainty of equity.

Bridgewater's security challenges — protecting $150B in assets and proprietary strategies — are uniquely compelling, but I'd need a base of $[X] and a guaranteed first-year bonus of $[X] to match."

3. **Certifications and Clearances:** "I hold [CISSP/OSCP/relevant clearances] and have [X] years of experience protecting financial infrastructure. These specialized qualifications support a base of $[X]."
