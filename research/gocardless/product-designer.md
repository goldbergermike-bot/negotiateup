**Negotiation DNA**

Product Designers at GoCardless shape the experience layer of a global bank-to-bank payment network used by 85,000+ businesses. Unlike consumer fintech design where engagement metrics dominate, GoCardless design work is measured by conversion rates on payment flows, merchant dashboard usability, and the clarity of complex financial information. The company is privately held at ~$2.1B, backed by Bain Capital, Accel, and Google Ventures, and the February 2026 $200M secondary sale demonstrates that the business is maturing toward potential public market readiness.

The Nuapay acquisition has created a significant design integration challenge. Two product experiences — GoCardless's direct debit platform and Nuapay's open banking interface — need to be unified into a coherent design system. Product Designers are central to this work, defining the visual language and interaction patterns that will carry the combined platform forward. VRP (Variable Recurring Payment) flows in particular require careful UX design, as they introduce new consent management patterns and payment authorization experiences that must meet both regulatory requirements and merchant expectations. Designing payment flows where errors have real financial consequences is a specialized skill, and GoCardless competes for this talent against companies like Stripe, Wise, and Adyen.
