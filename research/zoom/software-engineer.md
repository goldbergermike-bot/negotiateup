**Negotiation DNA**
Software Engineers at Zoom (IC1/IC2 level) build features for one of the world's most widely recognized technology products. Every line of code you write potentially affects hundreds of millions of users who rely on Zoom for daily communication. As an SWE at Zoom, you might work on meeting features, Zoom Phone call management, Team Chat messaging, AI Companion integrations, or platform infrastructure. The engineering challenges are real — real-time communication systems have performance constraints that most SaaS applications never encounter.

Zoom compensates SWEs at competitive Bay Area rates, with total comp of $185K-$295K for Bay Area roles. This positions Zoom above most mid-cap SaaS companies, reflecting both the San Jose location and the company's strong brand that attracts competitive talent. The RSU component is substantial — Zoom uses equity heavily, with RSUs typically comprising 30-40% of total comp. Zoom's stock has been recovering from post-pandemic corrections, and the AI narrative provides potential upside for equity-heavy packages.

Zoom's engineering culture emphasizes quality and craftsmanship. The company was built on the reputation of having the best, most reliable video quality in the market, and this quality-first mentality permeates engineering culture. SWEs are expected to write clean, performant code and take ownership of their features' reliability. For engineers who take pride in building high-quality software, Zoom's culture is a strong fit.

**Zoom UCaaS Platform Premium**
Software Engineers who bring experience with real-time systems, communication protocols, or media processing technologies have a meaningful advantage at Zoom. While Zoom hires strong generalists, engineers who understand the unique constraints of real-time software (latency sensitivity, packet loss handling, concurrent user scaling) bring immediate value. If you have experience with WebRTC, SIP protocols, or audio/video processing, these skills are directly relevant and negotiable.

Engineers from other communication platforms (Twilio, Vonage, RingCentral, Slack) or media companies (Netflix, Spotify, YouTube) bring transferable experience with real-time or streaming architectures. Even without direct communication experience, engineers with strong systems programming skills (C/C++, concurrency, low-level optimization) have an advantage for Zoom's performance-critical codebase.
