**Negotiation DNA**
Data Engineers at Zoom build the data infrastructure that powers both internal analytics and product-facing AI features for a platform handling billions of meeting minutes monthly. Zoom generates massive volumes of real-time communication data — meeting metadata, audio transcription streams, chat interactions, engagement signals, and quality-of-service metrics. Data Engineers architect the pipelines that ingest, process, and serve this data for analytics, ML model training, and the AI Companion feature set.

Zoom compensates Data Engineers competitively within the upper mid-tier tech segment. The $22B market cap and San Jose headquarters drive Bay Area-competitive packages, with total comp typically 5-15% above mid-cap SaaS peers like DocuSign or HubSpot. The company has been investing heavily in data infrastructure as the foundation for its AI strategy — every AI Companion feature depends on robust data pipelines — which elevates Data Engineering from a support function to a strategic capability.

The data engineering challenges at Zoom are genuinely interesting: real-time streaming data from millions of concurrent meetings, massive-scale audio/video processing pipelines, and the data governance challenges of handling communication data across jurisdictions with varying privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, regional compliance). Data Engineers who enjoy working at the intersection of real-time processing and privacy-compliant data management thrive here.

**Zoom Real-Time Data Premium**
Data Engineers who bring experience with real-time streaming data architectures — Kafka, Flink, Spark Streaming — have strong leverage at Zoom. The platform's data needs are fundamentally real-time: meeting quality monitoring, live transcription pipelines, and real-time engagement analytics all require sub-second data processing. Engineers who have built similar real-time data systems for media, telecommunications, or streaming platforms bring directly transferable expertise.

If you also bring experience with ML data infrastructure — feature stores, training data pipelines, model monitoring data systems — this directly supports Zoom's AI Companion strategy and commands a premium. The intersection of real-time streaming data and ML infrastructure is the exact skillset Zoom needs for its next-generation data platform.
