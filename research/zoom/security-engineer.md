**Negotiation DNA**
Security Engineers at Zoom carry a unique weight of responsibility. Zoom's 2020 "Zoombombing" crisis and the subsequent security overhaul made security a board-level priority and a core brand attribute. The company invested hundreds of millions in security infrastructure, hired a world-class CISO, and implemented end-to-end encryption (E2EE). Security engineers now protect a platform that hosts the most sensitive conversations in business — executive board meetings, M&A discussions, legal proceedings, and government briefings. A security failure at Zoom would be front-page news globally.

Zoom compensates Security Engineers at a meaningful premium — typically 15-20% above standard SWE bands at equivalent levels. This reflects both the talent scarcity in cybersecurity and the existential importance of security to Zoom's brand and business. The company cannot afford security failures, and this urgency translates to strong compensation, organizational support, and career growth for security professionals.

Zoom's security challenges span real-time communication encryption (E2EE for meetings, phone, and chat), cloud infrastructure security, application security for a massive SaaS platform, identity and authentication systems, and increasingly, AI security (protecting AI Companion features from manipulation and ensuring privacy of AI-processed meeting data). Security engineers who can work across these domains are most valued.

**Zoom Communication Security Premium**
Security Engineers with expertise in real-time communication security — end-to-end encryption protocols, key management for ephemeral communication, secure media transport, or WebRTC security — have exceptional leverage. These skills are highly specialized and directly applicable to Zoom's core security requirements. Very few security professionals globally have deep experience with E2EE for real-time multi-party communication, and those who do are in extraordinary demand.

Engineers with experience in AI security — preventing prompt injection attacks on AI features, ensuring privacy of data processed by ML models, and securing AI inference pipelines — are in growing demand as Zoom's AI Companion processes more meeting content. The intersection of communication security and AI security is a frontier domain with very few experienced practitioners.
