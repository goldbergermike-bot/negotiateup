**Negotiation DNA**

This is L3Harris's signature engineering role -- Mission Systems Engineer. L3Harris is the world's leading defense electronics and communications company, formed from the 2019 merger of L3 Technologies and Harris Corporation. Mission Systems Engineers design, integrate, and test the electronic warfare, ISR (intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance), and communication systems that every branch of the military depends on. You build the sensor payloads, electronic countermeasures, and tactical communication networks that give US forces their technological edge. L3Harris is a $45B public company with stable, predictable compensation: solid base salary, 10% annual bonus, and RSUs that provide steady value. The culture is traditional defense: 40-45 hour weeks, strong work-life balance, predictable career progression, pension/401k matching, and long-term job stability. The tradeoff vs. tech companies: lower total comp but dramatically better work-life balance and stability. [Source: L3Harris Mission Systems Comp 2025-2026]

**What Makes This Role Unique**

The Mission Systems Engineer integrates complete defense electronic systems:
- **Electronic Warfare Systems:** You design and integrate EW systems that detect, jam, and defeat adversary radar and communications
- **ISR Sensor Integration:** You integrate multi-sensor payloads (radar, EO/IR, SIGINT) onto aircraft, ships, and ground vehicles
- **Tactical Communications:** You build the communication networks that connect warfighters across domains -- voice, data, satellite, and mesh networks
- **System-of-Systems Integration:** You integrate L3Harris subsystems into larger weapons platforms built by Lockheed, Boeing, and Northrop
- **Full Lifecycle Engineering:** You work across requirements, design, integration, test, and fielding -- a complete systems engineering lifecycle
