**Negotiation DNA**

Product Designers at PwC work across the firm's digital experience consulting practice, Digital Accelerator program, and internal product teams. PwC's design practice is smaller than Deloitte Digital or Accenture Song but growing as the firm invests in digital product delivery capabilities. Designers create enterprise products for clients and internal tools used by PwC's 350,000+ employees. PwC's comp for design roles is lower than tech companies, with 10-20% bonus and limited equity at senior levels. The value proposition is regulated industry design experience — designing for financial services, healthcare, and government clients. [Source: PwC Digital Experience Practice 2025-2026]

**Global Levers**

1. **Digital Accelerator Innovation:** "Designing for the Digital Accelerator means working in a startup-like environment inside a Big 4 firm. This innovation velocity is unusual for PwC."
2. **Regulated Industry Design:** "Designing for audit, tax, and compliance tools requires specialized UX expertise. Few designers have this regulated-industry background."
3. **Tech Company Design Comp Gap:** "Product designers at Google, Figma, and Airbnb earn $200K-$320K TC. PwC's gap needs to be addressed through base and bonus."
4. **Firm-Wide Impact:** "My designs are used by 350,000+ PwC employees. The scale of internal product design exceeds most tech company design roles."
