Revolut's trajectory to **$9B in revenue** and **$3.5B in profit** is a product story as much as an engineering one. #### Global Levers

**Lever 1: Revenue-Attribution Equity Premium**
> "As a Product Manager owning [payments/subscriptions/trading], my decisions directly impact the $9B revenue target. I'd like to discuss an option grant that reflects this revenue attribution — PMs in revenue-critical product areas should be compensated at the top of the equity band, given the direct line between my roadmap and the IPO financial narrative."

**Lever 2: Super-App Scope Premium**
> "Revolut's super-app model means PMs manage product areas that would be standalone companies elsewhere — payments, crypto, insurance, credit. The breadth of scope justifies compensation at the higher end of the PM band. At a single-product fintech, I'd own 100% of the product; at Revolut, I own a $1B+ revenue line within a $9B platform."

**Lever 3: Competing Offer — Public Company RSU Comparison**
> "I have an offer from [Stripe/Wise/public tech company] with $X in liquid RSUs. Revolut's options are illiquid with a 4-year vesting schedule. To make the transition financially rational, I'd expect a 1.5x equity premium over the public-company offer, reflecting the illiquidity discount and the pre-IPO upside embedded in Revolut's $75B valuation."

**Lever 4: Product Area Assignment Leverage**
> "I'd like to understand which product area this role maps to before finalizing the offer. PMs owning core revenue products (payments, premium subscriptions) versus growth/experimental products have meaningfully different impact profiles. If the role is in a core revenue area, the comp should reflect that strategic importance."
