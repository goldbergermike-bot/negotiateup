#### Negotiation DNA

DevOps Engineers at CME Group are responsible for the operational backbone of the world's largest derivatives marketplace. You build and maintain the CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, monitoring systems, and deployment workflows that keep CME's four exchanges — CME, CBOT, NYMEX, and COMEX — running at 99.999%+ uptime while processing 6.4 billion contracts annually. At CME, downtime is measured not in lost revenue but in market disruption — every second of exchange unavailability impacts global derivatives pricing and represents regulatory risk. The operational standards are among the most demanding in technology.

February 2026 introduced an entirely new operational domain for DevOps Engineers at CME Group. The shift from "selling data" to "hydrating models" requires a parallel infrastructure stack: the model-hydration platform demands its own CI/CD pipelines, data pipeline orchestration, model serving infrastructure, monitoring for data quality and latency SLAs, and deployment automation for high-fidelity data feeds. DevOps Engineers who build the operational layer of the model-hydration platform are working on CME's most strategically important new infrastructure, and they command a 15-20% premium under CME's "High-Fidelity Data" pay bands.

CEO Terry Duffy's technology-forward strategy has increased investment in DevOps and infrastructure automation across CME. The model-hydration initiative effectively doubles the operational surface area, creating significant demand for DevOps Engineers who can operate at the intersection of exchange-grade reliability and ML-platform operations. CME's public equity (NASDAQ: CME) ensures your RSU grants carry meaningful upside.

#### Model Hydration — The High-Fidelity Data Premium

- **The Feb 2026 Strategic Shift:** In February 2026, CME Group transitioned from traditional data licensing to "hydrating models" — building high-fidelity data pipelines for AI/ML model training and inference. DevOps Engineers building the operational infrastructure for model-hydration pipelines — CI/CD, monitoring, orchestration, and deployment automation — are critical to this transformation. DevOps Engineers on model-hydration infrastructure see total comp packages $27K-$52K above standard DevOps bands.

- **High-Fidelity Data Pay Bands:** CME has established "High-Fidelity Data" pay bands for DevOps Engineers working on model-hydration platform infrastructure. At the DevOps level, this translates to a 15-20% premium — pushing the Chicago ceiling from $262K to approximately $301K-$314K for engineers with demonstrated experience in ML-platform operations, data pipeline orchestration, or streaming-infrastructure DevOps.

- **Why CME's Data Is Uniquely Valuable:** CME's derivatives data — options pricing, volatility surfaces, order book depth, trade flow, and Greeks — requires operational infrastructure that guarantees data completeness, sub-millisecond latency monitoring, and zero-loss delivery to AI model training systems. DevOps Engineers who build the operational guarantees around this data are ensuring that AI models receive the highest-fidelity financial data in existence.

- **Frame Yourself as the Model-Hydration Operations Architect:** In negotiations, position yourself as "the engineer who builds the operational guarantees for CME's model-hydration infrastructure." Emphasize experience with data pipeline orchestration (Airflow, Prefect, Dagster), streaming infrastructure operations (Kafka, Flink), or ML-platform DevOps (MLflow, Kubeflow). This framing connects your operational skills to CME's Feb 2026 strategic shift.
