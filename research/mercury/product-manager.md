**Negotiation DNA**

Product managers at Mercury define the banking experience for 200K+ startups and SMBs. This is not a standard B2B SaaS PM role — you're managing a financial product where every feature decision has regulatory implications, where users trust you with their company's operating capital, and where a bad product decision can trigger compliance violations. Mercury PMs must understand banking regulations, payment rails, and financial operations alongside traditional product management skills.

Mercury's product differentiation comes from exceptional UX applied to historically terrible banking experiences. PMs are expected to have strong product taste and design sensibility — Mercury doesn't ship "good enough" features. The 2026 product roadmap centers on AI-powered financial tools, credit products, and international expansion. PMs who can navigate both the product vision and the regulatory constraints of these new domains are rare. [Source: Mercury Product Team, Careers 2025-2026]

This is foundational product strategy, not incremental feature work.
