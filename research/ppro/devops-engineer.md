#### PPRO LPM Expansion & Regional Impact Lever

The **Scalapay Southern Europe partnership (February 2026)** creates urgent DevOps challenges. Expanding LPM coverage across Southern Europe means scaling infrastructure to handle new payment method connectors, ensuring data residency compliance across Italian, French, Spanish, and Portuguese jurisdictions, and maintaining payment-grade reliability during rapid integration rollouts.

For DevOps Engineers, the Regional Impact is operationally critical:
- **Multi-region infrastructure**: Scaling PPRO's platform to handle Scalapay BNPL traffic across Southern European markets with distinct data residency requirements
- **LPM deployment velocity**: Each new local payment method connector requires deployment pipeline support — the Scalapay expansion accelerates the pace of new LPM rollouts
- **Payment-grade reliability**: BNPL and LPM infrastructure has zero tolerance for downtime during checkout — a failed transaction is a lost sale for the merchant and reputational risk for PPRO's entire partner network
- **LPM fragmentation at the infrastructure layer**: Managing 100+ payment method connectors means managing 100+ sets of infrastructure dependencies, monitoring configurations, and failover procedures
- **Regional Impact framing**: Frame your work as "ensuring the operational reliability of PPRO's LPM expansion across Southern Europe" — this connects DevOps work to the company's strategic narrative

The complexity premium of managing infrastructure for regulatory, technical, and cultural differences across 100+ payment methods is substantial for DevOps roles.
