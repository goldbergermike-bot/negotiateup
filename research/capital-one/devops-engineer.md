**Negotiation DNA**

Capital One was the first major bank to migrate entirely to AWS, making it a model for cloud-native banking infrastructure. DevOps Engineers at Capital One build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, Kubernetes clusters, infrastructure-as-code, and observability platforms on AWS at massive scale. The company's cloud-first approach means DevOps engineers work with cutting-edge cloud tools and practices rather than legacy on-premise infrastructure, creating a more attractive technical environment than peer banks.

Compensation follows the tech model: base plus RSUs (4-year vest) plus bonus (10-15%). DevOps compensation is competitive with AWS, Google Cloud, and infrastructure companies. Capital One's McLean VA location provides significant cost-of-living advantage, making total comp stretch further than equivalent packages in the Bay Area or NYC.

Competition comes from AWS (a close partner), Google Cloud, Azure, infrastructure companies (HashiCorp, Datadog), and other tech companies. Capital One's advantage is the combination of cloud-native banking infrastructure at massive scale -- a unique DevOps challenge that blends cutting-edge cloud engineering with financial services requirements.

Capital One's cloud-native infrastructure means DevOps engineers work on problems that are unique in financial services: securing cloud banking infrastructure at scale, implementing compliance-as-code for financial regulations, and building resilient systems that meet banking uptime requirements on public cloud.
