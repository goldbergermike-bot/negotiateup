#### Negotiation DNA

Robinhood Security Engineers (IC3-IC4) protect the financial assets, personal data, and platform integrity of a system serving 24M+ funded accounts with billions of dollars in customer assets. You design and implement the security architecture, threat detection systems, incident response procedures, and compliance frameworks that ensure Robinhood meets SOC2, SEC, FINRA, PCI-DSS, and state regulatory security requirements. Your security decisions are not abstract — a breach at Robinhood would expose real people's money, investment portfolios, Social Security numbers, and financial histories. This is tier-one security responsibility, comparable to bank and exchange security roles, and your compensation should reflect the asset value under your protection.

The Rothera JV dramatically expands the security attack surface. As Robinhood transforms into an "Information and Capital Markets Ecosystem," Security Engineers must protect six or more product verticals — each with distinct threat models, compliance requirements, and data sensitivity profiles. Crypto wallets have different security requirements than retirement accounts, which differ from credit card systems, which differ from institutional data feeds. Security Engineers who can architect protection across this multi-vertical attack surface are extraordinarily scarce and can negotiate Ecosystem-Critical premiums of $22K-$40K above standard offers.

In the post-breach fintech landscape, security engineering is not a cost center — it is the trust foundation the entire business depends on. A single security incident can destroy billions in market cap, trigger regulatory sanctions, and permanently damage user trust. Price your compensation as the insurance premium Robinhood pays against catastrophic loss.

#### Rothera — The Ecosystem-Critical Premium

The mid-2026 Rothera Joint Venture transforms Robinhood from a commission-free trading application into an "Information and Capital Markets Ecosystem." For Security Engineers, this creates a dramatically expanded threat surface — and a corresponding compensation premium:

- **Ecosystem Security Architects Command $22K-$40K Premiums:** Security Engineers designing protection across multiple product verticals — unified identity and access management, cross-product threat detection, ecosystem-wide encryption standards, multi-vertical compliance frameworks — are classified as ecosystem-critical. These roles carry a $22K-$40K annual premium because a security failure in any one vertical can cascade across the entire ecosystem.

- **6-Vertical Threat Surface Is the Most Complex in Consumer Fintech:** Post-Rothera, Robinhood's threat model spans equities trading (SEC-regulated), crypto wallets (state money transmitter-regulated), retirement accounts (ERISA-governed), credit cards (PCI-DSS), cash management (FDIC-insured banking), and institutional data (contractual SLAs). Security Engineers who can architect protection across all six verticals simultaneously justify $300K-$328K TC at the IC4 level.

- **Asset Value Under Protection Justifies Premium Comp of $15K-$25K:** Robinhood holds billions of dollars in customer assets across equities, crypto, and cash accounts. The value of assets under your security protection should be a direct input into your compensation negotiation. Security Engineers protecting $10B+ in customer assets at other financial institutions command $15K-$25K premiums over engineers protecting non-financial systems.

- **Regulatory Security Compliance Is a $12K-$20K Scarcity Premium:** The Rothera ecosystem must satisfy security requirements from SEC, FINRA, banking regulators, state money transmitter authorities, and PCI-DSS simultaneously. Security Engineers who can design frameworks meeting all of these requirements concurrently — not sequentially — are among the rarest in the industry. This multi-regulatory security expertise adds $12K-$20K to your negotiable range. Negotiate sign-on bonuses of $25K-$45K.
