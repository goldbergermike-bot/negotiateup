---

Atlassian (NASDAQ: TEAM, $4.4B+ ARR) powers collaboration for over 300,000 enterprise customers and 10M+ monthly active users through Jira, Confluence, Trello, Bitbucket, Jira Service Management, and Loom. The Senior Software Engineer is the primary IC delivery engine at Atlassian -- owning system design, code quality, and technical mentorship within a pod. Since the Feb 2, 2026 rollout of Atlassian Guard Premium and the Rovo AI engine, Senior SWEs are expected to serve as AI Orchestra conductors: orchestrating Rovo AI agents to automate enterprise workflows while simultaneously designing the Guard Premium security governance architecture that constrains those agents. Compensation is no-bonus, RSU-heavy (Atlassian RSUs -- NASDAQ: TEAM), making equity the core negotiation surface.

#### Negotiation DNA

Senior Software Engineers at Atlassian are the backbone of product velocity. At P3-P4, you own technical design decisions, drive architecture reviews, and mentor junior engineers. The Feb 2, 2026 rollout of Guard Premium and Rovo AI fundamentally expanded the Senior SWE remit: you are now an AI Orchestra conductor managing the Conductor/Instrument dual role. As Conductor, you design how Rovo AI agents interact with Atlassian products -- automating workflows, routing tasks, generating content. As Instrument, you build and enforce Guard Premium security governance policies that control what these agents can access, modify, and execute. This dual capability commands premium Atlassian RSUs (NASDAQ: TEAM) in the no-bonus comp model.

### AI Orchestra Lever

The Feb 2, 2026 rollout of Atlassian Guard Premium and the Rovo AI engine created a new paradigm for Senior SWEs. You are now expected to operate as an AI Orchestra conductor -- playing the dual Conductor/Instrument role that defines Atlassian's Agentic Future strategy. As Conductor, you orchestrate Rovo AI agents across the product suite, designing agent workflows that automate Jira issue routing, Confluence content generation, and cross-product task coordination. As Instrument, you build the Guard Premium security governance layer that ensures these autonomous agents operate within enterprise compliance boundaries -- data access controls, audit trails, and policy enforcement.

For Senior SWEs, this means you own both the intelligence layer (Rovo) and the trust layer (Guard Premium). Frame your experience: "I've designed systems where autonomous agents operate within strict governance frameworks -- this is the exact Conductor/Instrument duality that the AI Orchestra model requires. My experience maps directly to the post-Feb 2, 2026 Guard Premium and Rovo architecture."

#### Global Levers

1. **No Bonus = All Negotiation Goes to Atlassian RSUs (NASDAQ: TEAM).** Zero bonus means equity is the only variable lever. Push for 25-35% above the initial RSU offer: "With no bonus at Atlassian, I need TEAM RSUs to compensate. My competing offer at [Google/Datadog] includes a 15-20% bonus. I'm asking for $220K in Atlassian RSUs over 4 years to reach total comp parity."

2. **Guard Premium + Rovo AI Orchestra Positioning (Feb 2, 2026 Rollout).** Position yourself as a ready-made AI Orchestra conductor post the Feb 2, 2026 Guard Premium/Rovo rollout: "I have direct experience building autonomous agent systems with governance controls. The Conductor/Instrument duality -- orchestrating Rovo AI agents while enforcing Guard Premium security policies -- is exactly what my background delivers. This AI Orchestra capability is what Atlassian needs for its Agentic Future."

3. **System Design Ownership at Scale.** Senior SWEs own architecture for systems serving millions of users: "I've designed distributed systems handling [X]M requests/day. At Atlassian, this maps to owning architecture across Jira, Confluence, and the cloud platform -- driving both reliability and the AI agent integration layer."

4. **Team Anywhere Premium.** Atlassian pays the same within a country regardless of location. Use this defensively: "Atlassian's Team Anywhere policy is a competitive advantage for me, but it also means my comp should be benchmarked against top-of-market SF rates, not discounted for geography."
