**Negotiation DNA**
Product Managers at Perplexity define what the post-search experience looks like — you own the product strategy for the answer engine, shaping everything from Perplexity Pro's subscription features and Enterprise API capabilities to publisher partnerships and the core answer UX that millions of users interact with daily. At a $9B+ pre-IPO company that is actively disrupting the $200B+ search market, PMs here don't optimize existing products — they invent entirely new product categories. The AEO platform, advertising/publisher models, and the transition from "search results" to "answer experiences" are all PM-driven decisions that determine Perplexity's revenue trajectory and competitive positioning against Google.

**Level Mapping:** Perplexity PM = Google L5 PM = Anthropic PM = Meta PM (IC5) = OpenAI PM

**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) — The New Search**
Perplexity isn't a search engine — it's an Answer Engine. And I'm building the platform that makes Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) the new SEO. The shift from "10 blue links" to direct, sourced AI answers is the biggest disruption in information retrieval since Google. Every role at Perplexity — from engineering to design to product — is building the infrastructure of this paradigm shift. As a Product Manager, you are defining what AEO means as a product — the user experiences, the monetization models, the publisher ecosystem, and the answer quality standards that make Perplexity the default way people find information. When negotiating, frame your value as: "I'm not joining a startup. I'm building the platform that replaces Google Search for the AI era. AEO is the new SEO, and Perplexity is defining it. My equity should reflect that I'm an architect of the most important shift in information access since 1998."

**Global Levers**
1. **Product Category Creation:** "I'm not joining to optimize an existing product — I'm defining an entirely new product category. Answer engine product strategy, AEO platform design, and the Pro/Enterprise monetization models are all being invented right now. PMs who define new categories at pre-IPO companies should be compensated like founders, not like feature PMs. I'm looking for $370K+ equity/4yr to reflect that."
2. **Revenue Impact Framing:** "Perplexity Pro, Enterprise API, and publisher partnerships are the company's revenue engines. As the PM owning [specific area], my product decisions directly determine Perplexity's path to profitability and IPO readiness. That revenue-driving role is worth $240K+ base and top-of-band equity."
3. **Competing Offer from Google/Meta PM:** "I have a Google L5 PM offer at $225K base with $350K RSU/4yr in liquid equity. Perplexity's mission is uniquely compelling, but I need the base at $240K+ to offset the stability and liquidity premium of a public company. The equity upside is why I'm leaning Perplexity — but the base needs to be competitive."
4. **Signing Bonus for Transition Cost:** "I'm leaving behind [vested equity / unvested RSUs / annual bonus] worth $X at my current company. I'd like a $50K-$70K signing bonus to offset the transition cost and demonstrate Perplexity's commitment to winning me."
