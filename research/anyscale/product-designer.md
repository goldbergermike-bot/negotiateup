**Negotiation DNA**
Product Designers at Anyscale create the interfaces that make distributed AI infrastructure accessible and manageable — cluster management dashboards, job monitoring views, resource utilization visualizations, cost analysis tools, and the workflow UIs that help enterprise teams operate distributed Ray workloads without needing deep distributed systems expertise. The design challenge is making extraordinarily complex infrastructure feel simple and actionable.

This is infrastructure UX design — one of the most specialized and technically demanding design disciplines. You must understand distributed computing concepts, GPU utilization patterns, and cloud infrastructure terminology well enough to design interfaces that are both technically accurate and intuitively usable. Companies like Datadog, Grafana, and Vercel compete for designers with this infrastructure visualization and developer experience expertise.

### AI Infrastructure Developer Experience Design Lever
Anyscale's 2026 design challenge is making the managed Ray platform feel as accessible as a simple cloud service — even though the underlying distributed computing infrastructure is enormously complex. Product Designers must create interfaces for cluster provisioning, job submission, GPU resource management, distributed training monitoring, and cost optimization that enterprise ML teams can use without hiring distributed systems experts.

The AI workload visualization dimension adds new design challenges: designers must create interfaces that help users understand training progress, model quality metrics, serving performance, and resource utilization across potentially thousands of distributed workers. These visualization patterns don't exist in standard infrastructure design — they must be invented for the distributed AI workload context.
