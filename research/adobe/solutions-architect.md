## Year 2 Refresh Strategy

Adobe's refresh culture is your biggest negotiation lever. Rather than fighting over $20K on the initial grant, negotiate a WRITTEN commitment to a Year 2 refresh. Use this script:

> *"I understand Adobe's initial grants are standard-band. What I'd like is a written commitment to a Year 2 refresh target of 80-100% of my initial grant, contingent on meeting expectations. This gives me confidence that my comp trajectory will be competitive."*

**Why this works for Solutions Architects at Adobe:**

Adobe's initial RSU grant is $140K-$238K over 4 years. Rather than fighting for $20K more on the initial grant, negotiate a WRITTEN commitment to a Year 2 refresh target of 80-100% of your initial grant. Adobe's refresh culture is strong — a guaranteed Year 2 refresh of $112K-$238K is worth more than $20K extra on the initial grant because it sets the trajectory for Years 3 and 4.

**Example calculation:**
- Initial grant: $190K/4yr = $47.5K/year vesting
- Year 2 refresh at 85%: $161.5K/4yr = additional $40.4K/year vesting starting Year 2
- By Year 3, you're vesting $87.9K/year instead of $47.5K/year — that's the compound power of refresh negotiation
- Over a 4-year stay, the refresh commitment adds $121K+ in cumulative equity value

Solutions Architects are directly tied to enterprise customer success and revenue retention. Adobe invests heavily in SA retention because customer relationships are built on SA trust and continuity. Adobe managers have refresh budget discretion — getting this commitment in writing ensures your Years 2-4 comp grows, not stagnates.

## Global Negotiation Levers

**1. Base Salary Lever**
The SA band ($162K-$202K in San Jose) has $40K of range. Adobe benchmarks SA comp against Salesforce Solutions Architects, AWS Professional Services, and Google Cloud Customer Engineers. Your base salary argument should emphasize customer-facing technical leadership — if you've designed and delivered enterprise implementations for Fortune 500 companies, quantify the contract values involved. SAs who've worked with Adobe's Experience Cloud stack at prior roles or consulting firms (Accenture, Deloitte, Publicis Sapient) command upper-band placement.

**2. RSU Grant Lever**
The $140K-$238K RSU range is meaningful. SA equity grants at Adobe vary by the revenue portfolio they'll support. SAs supporting Strategic/Enterprise accounts (multi-million dollar ACV deals) receive higher grants than those supporting mid-market. If you know your target customer segment, use this in negotiation. SAs joining the Experience Cloud platform team (AEP architecture) or Firefly AI enterprise integration roles command premium equity.

**3. Signing Bonus Lever**
SA signing bonuses at Adobe range from $18K-$38K. If you're coming from a consulting background (Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey Implementation), you may be forfeiting significant performance bonuses or partner-track comp. Present this forfeiture clearly. If you're leaving another tech SA role (Salesforce, AWS, Google), quantify unvested equity and retention bonuses. Adobe's recruiting team understands SA compensation structures and will work to bridge the gap.

**4. Location & Transfer Lever**
Solutions Architect roles at Adobe are primarily in San Jose and London, with a growing presence in Bangalore. London SAs support the EMEA enterprise customer base and often receive a location premium to retain talent that could otherwise join consulting firms. Bangalore SAs increasingly support APAC enterprise customers. SAs who are willing to travel to customer sites (common in the role) and work across regional hubs demonstrate flexibility that supports premium comp positioning.

---

> **Negotiate Up Strategy:** Solutions Architects at Adobe are revenue protectors — your architectural expertise directly impacts customer retention and expansion revenue. Frame your negotiation around the revenue you'll influence: base salary push to $185K+ (cite Salesforce/AWS SA comp), RSU above $190K (anchor to your enterprise account portfolio), and a written Year 2 refresh commitment. The SA refresh at Adobe is strong because customer continuity depends on SA retention — Adobe would rather invest in your comp trajectory than risk customer relationships. A well-negotiated SA package with a refresh commitment can add $60K-$120K to your cumulative 4-year compensation.
