**Negotiation DNA**

Technical program managers at Gusto coordinate the company's most complex cross-functional initiatives — the platform modernization from monolith to microservices, international payroll expansion, and AI integration across all product lines. As of February 2026, Gusto has approximately 15 TPMs, making it a lean team relative to the company's engineering headcount of 600+. Each TPM owns programs that span 4-8 engineering teams, and the role carries significant organizational influence.

Gusto's TPM comp is competitive with mid-tier tech companies, with equity representing meaningful upside. The bonus component (15-20%) is tied to program delivery milestones and is consistently achievable for strong performers. TPMs with experience in fintech, platform migrations, or international product launches command a premium — Gusto's simultaneous execution of all three creates exceptional demand for TPMs who can manage complexity at this scale.

### Platform Migration Program Lever
Gusto's monolith-to-microservices migration is the largest technical program in the company's history — spanning all engineering teams, requiring 18-24 months, and serving as a prerequisite for IPO readiness. The program has a dedicated TPM lead, and the role carries direct executive visibility (weekly updates to CTO and VP Engineering). Successfully delivering this migration is career-defining work.

TPMs who have led similar platform migrations at fintech or financial services companies are exceptionally rare. The combination of technical depth (understanding service decomposition) and program management rigor (managing dependencies across 20+ teams) narrows the talent pool significantly.
