**Negotiation DNA**

The AI Payroll Platform Engineer role is Gusto's most strategically critical technical position in 2026. This role sits at the intersection of ML infrastructure, payroll processing, and regulatory compliance — building the production platform that powers Gusto's entire AI payroll automation suite. Unlike a general ML engineer who builds individual models, the AI Payroll Platform Engineer designs and maintains the end-to-end ML serving infrastructure that makes AI-powered payroll possible at scale: feature stores for payroll data, real-time inference pipelines, model versioning and rollback systems, and compliance-grade audit logging.

As of February 2026, Gusto has only 3-4 engineers in this specific role, making it one of the scarcest positions in the company. The role requires a rare combination of ML platform engineering (MLOps, model serving, feature engineering), financial systems expertise (exactly-once processing, regulatory audit trails), and payroll domain knowledge. Gusto's CEO has publicly stated that AI payroll automation is the company's "single largest competitive moat," and this role is the foundation of that moat.

Comp for this role exceeds standard ML engineering bands by 15-25%, reflecting both the scarcity of qualified candidates and the strategic importance of the work. Equity is the primary lever — Gusto recognizes that AI platform engineers with fintech experience are being courted by every major tech company.

### AI Payroll Automation Moat Lever
Gusto's AI payroll automation platform is the technical foundation for everything the company builds in 2026 and beyond. The platform currently handles: automated tax classification (50 states, 8 international markets), predictive compliance alerts (catching errors before payroll runs), intelligent benefits optimization, and the AI Payroll Copilot product. Each of these features runs on the ML serving infrastructure that AI Payroll Platform Engineers build and maintain.

The platform processes inference requests during payroll runs that cover $150B+ in annual payroll volume. A single platform failure during a payroll run could affect millions of workers. This extreme reliability requirement, combined with the need for sub-100ms inference latency, makes the role significantly more demanding than standard ML platform engineering. Gusto cannot ship new AI features without expanding this team, creating a direct bottleneck that gives you extraordinary leverage.
