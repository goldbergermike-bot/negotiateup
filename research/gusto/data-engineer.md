**Negotiation DNA**

Data engineers at Gusto build and maintain the pipelines that power payroll processing, tax calculations, compliance reporting, and the Gusto Insights product. As of February 2026, Gusto's data platform processes over 10 billion payroll events annually across 400,000+ businesses. The data engineering team has been expanding to support real-time data requirements — payroll errors need to be caught in real-time, not in batch — and the Gusto Insights product requires near-real-time anonymized data aggregation.

Gusto's data engineering comp reflects the fintech premium: payroll data pipelines must be accurate to the penny, compliant with state and federal regulations, and available 24/7 during payroll cycles. Data engineers with experience in financial-grade data systems (exactly-once processing, audit logging, data lineage tracking) command a premium. The team primarily uses Apache Spark, Airflow, dbt, and Snowflake, with a growing investment in real-time streaming via Kafka.

### Real-Time Payroll Data Platform Lever
Gusto is migrating from batch-oriented payroll processing to a real-time streaming architecture. This transformation enables instant payroll previews, real-time tax calculations, and same-day payroll corrections — features that differentiate Gusto from legacy providers like ADP and Paychex. The real-time platform initiative has a $15M annual infrastructure budget and is one of Gusto's top three engineering investments for 2026.

Data engineers who have built real-time streaming systems at scale — particularly in financial or compliance-sensitive contexts — are the scarcest talent for this initiative. If you've worked with Kafka, Flink, or similar streaming technologies in a fintech environment, you have strong leverage to negotiate above-band compensation.
