### Settlement Platform Technical Deep-Dive: What You Will Build

Understanding the technical scope of this role strengthens your negotiation by demonstrating domain depth. Be prepared to discuss these components in your negotiation conversations:

**Settlement Graph Engine:** The routing logic that determines how a transaction moves from authorization through stablecoin settlement to merchant payout. This must handle multi-currency conversion, jurisdiction-specific settlement rules, and fallback paths when the stablecoin rail is unavailable. The settlement graph operates across Finaro's global acquiring markets and must produce deterministic settlement outcomes with full auditability.

**Stablecoin Reserve Manager:** The system that manages Shift4's stablecoin pool — minting, redeeming, and rebalancing stablecoins to maintain optimal liquidity for 24/7 Global Liquidity. This includes integration with stablecoin issuers, banking partners for fiat on/off ramps, and the treasury optimization algorithms that minimize idle capital.

**Real-Time Reconciliation Engine:** A system that continuously matches blockchain settlement events to merchant ledger entries, identifying and resolving discrepancies in real-time. Unlike traditional end-of-day reconciliation, this engine operates 24/7 beyond traditional banking hours, providing merchants and Shift4 operations with continuous settlement status.

**Settlement Monitoring & Alerting:** The observability layer that ensures 24/7 Global Liquidity actually operates 24/7. This includes settlement flow dashboards, anomaly detection for unusual settlement patterns, and automated incident response for settlement disruptions.

**Merchant Settlement API:** The API layer that merchants and integrated software vendors use to interact with the Stablecoin Settlement Platform — querying settlement status, configuring settlement preferences, and receiving webhook notifications for settlement events.
