#### Negotiation DNA

Security Engineers at Ally Financial protect the entire attack surface of a $196B+ financial institution that exists exclusively in digital space. Traditional banks distribute their security risk across physical branches (guarded by physical security), paper processes (protected by custody chains), and digital channels (defended by cybersecurity). Ally has no physical branches and no paper processes — 100% of Ally's security posture is cybersecurity. This means Security Engineers at Ally carry the full weight of protecting 11 million+ customers' financial assets, identities, and data in a purely digital environment.

The Ally.ai platform and Responsible AI Institute membership introduce security challenges that do not exist at most financial institutions. Security Engineers at Ally must defend against adversarial attacks on AI models — prompt injection, data poisoning, model extraction, and adversarial examples — in addition to traditional cybersecurity threats. As Ally.ai powers more automated decisions, the attack surface expands from traditional application security to include AI/ML model security. This "Empathetic Automation" security domain requires engineers who understand both classical cybersecurity and emerging AI security threats.

The regulatory environment amplifies the stakes. Ally's banking charter subjects it to OCC, FDIC, and Federal Reserve examination, and security failures can result in consent orders, fines, and charter risk. Security Engineers at Ally are not just protecting a product — they are protecting a regulated institution's license to operate.
