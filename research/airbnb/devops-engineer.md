**Negotiation DNA**

DevOps Engineers at Airbnb maintain the global infrastructure that powers a marketplace operating in 220+ countries with extreme seasonal traffic patterns. Airbnb's infrastructure must handle massive traffic spikes (New Year's Eve, summer booking season, major events) while maintaining sub-second response times for search, booking, and payments. The 2026 Experiences expansion adds entirely new infrastructure requirements: real-time availability for curated activities, AI trip planning compute workloads, and a second marketplace with distinct scaling characteristics. Airbnb's no-bonus comp structure means equity is your primary lever. DevOps engineers who can demonstrate experience with global-scale infrastructure, multi-region deployments, and marketplace reliability are compensated at the top of band. **Live Anywhere + Experiences Marketplace**

"Airbnb's 'Live and Work Anywhere' policy means you can work from any location — and earn the same compensation regardless of where you live. This flexibility is worth $20K-$50K in effective purchasing power if you choose a lower-cost location. Combined with Airbnb's expansion into Experiences — curated activities, AI-powered trip planning, and the next $5B marketplace — every role is building both the accommodation platform AND the Experiences ecosystem. When negotiating: 'I'm building the dual marketplace — accommodations AND Experiences — that makes Airbnb a comprehensive travel platform. The Live Anywhere flexibility is a tangible financial advantage worth $20K-$50K, and the Experiences expansion means my work drives the next $5B in revenue.'"

**Global Levers**

1. **Global Scale Frame:** "Airbnb operates in 220+ countries with 7M+ active listings. The infrastructure I'm managing serves traffic from every continent, handles seasonal spikes of 3-5x normal load, and must maintain sub-second latency for search and booking. This is global-scale infrastructure work, not single-region operations."
2. **Dual-Marketplace Infrastructure:** The Experiences expansion creates a second set of infrastructure requirements — activity availability, real-time booking for time-sensitive experiences, AI inference infrastructure for trip planning. Frame: "I'm building infrastructure for two marketplaces, not one. Experiences has different latency, availability, and compute requirements than accommodations."
3. **AI Compute Infrastructure:** AI trip planning requires GPU/TPU inference infrastructure, model serving, and LLM orchestration at scale. Frame: "The AI trip planning initiative requires production ML infrastructure — model serving, inference optimization, and cost management. I bring [X] experience scaling AI workloads."
4. **Uptime Revenue Impact:** Every minute of downtime costs Airbnb millions in lost bookings. Frame: "Infrastructure reliability directly determines Airbnb's revenue. A single hour of downtime during peak booking season costs $[X]M. My track record of maintaining [X]% uptime at [company] translates to direct revenue protection."
