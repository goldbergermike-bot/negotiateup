**Negotiation DNA**
Product Designers at Workiva face one of the most challenging design briefs in enterprise software: making regulatory compliance feel intuitive. The platform helps users create SEC filings, manage SOX controls, produce ESG reports, and coordinate audit workflows — processes that are inherently complex, heavily regulated, and high-stakes. A well-designed compliance workflow saves enterprises hours of manual effort and reduces the risk of costly regulatory errors. A poorly designed one creates confusion and compliance risk.

Workiva compensates Product Designers in line with its Midwest-based compensation philosophy, below Bay Area design salaries but competitive for the GRC sector and for remote-first positions. The purchasing power in lower-COL areas is strong, and Workiva's work-life balance culture means designers can maintain sustainable productivity without burnout.

The design team at Workiva works on genuinely interesting challenges: real-time collaborative document editing (similar in complexity to Google Docs), data visualization for compliance metrics, workflow automation interfaces, and increasingly, AI-powered assistance features. Designers who enjoy making complex systems accessible will find Workiva's problems deeply engaging.

**Workiva Compliance UX Premium**
Product Designers who specialize in complex enterprise workflows — particularly in regulated or compliance-heavy domains — have leverage at Workiva. The platform requires designers who can handle information-dense interfaces, multi-step regulatory workflows, and collaborative editing experiences without oversimplifying away critical compliance details. This balance between simplicity and regulatory completeness is a specialized design skill.

Designers with experience in collaborative editing interfaces (Google Docs-style), data-rich dashboard design, or accessibility for enterprise applications bring directly transferable skills. If you've designed for financial services, legal, or audit professionals, you understand the conservative user expectations and precision requirements that Workiva's audience demands.
