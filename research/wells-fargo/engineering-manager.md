**Negotiation DNA**

Engineering Managers at Wells Fargo are leading the bank's technology culture transformation, building modern engineering teams and practices from the ground up. EMs (SVP or Director titles) are responsible not only for people management and technical delivery but also for establishing engineering standards, reducing contractor dependency, and creating the kind of engineering culture that attracts and retains tech-caliber talent. This is fundamentally a transformation leadership role, not just a management position.

Compensation is heavily bonus-weighted: base plus discretionary bonus (20-35% at SVP, 30-50%+ at Director) plus equity awards and deferred compensation. Wells Fargo has been recruiting engineering managers from Big Tech companies and offering competitive packages to change the bank's technology culture. EMs with experience transforming engineering organizations are particularly valued and can command above-band compensation.

Competition comes from Big Tech (all nearby in the Bay Area), other banks (JPMorgan, Capital One), and fintech companies. Wells Fargo's advantage is the transformation opportunity: EMs who join during the rebuilding have outsized impact and accelerated career growth.

**Level Mapping:** Engineering Manager at WF (SVP/Director) = L6-L7 at Google, M1-M2 at Meta, SDM-Principal SDM at Amazon, Director at Capital One, SVP/MD at JPMorgan

**The Culture Transformation Premium**

Engineering management at Wells Fargo is more challenging and more impactful than at established tech companies. EMs must transform teams that may include significant contractor ratios, legacy technology skills, and traditional waterfall practices into modern, agile engineering organizations. This transformation work -- building culture, establishing engineering excellence, and retaining talent -- is valued higher than steady-state management. Wells Fargo has shown willingness to pay 15-25% above standard bank EM bands for candidates who bring proven transformation experience from tech companies.
