**Negotiation DNA**

Data Scientists at Melio sit at the analytical core of a platform that has processed over $100B in B2B payments for 500,000+ small and medium businesses. The data generated by this volume — transaction patterns, payment timing, vendor relationships, cash-flow cycles, fraud signals — is extraordinarily rich and directly actionable. Unlike data science roles at companies where insights are advisory, Melio's data scientists build models that directly influence product decisions, risk assessments, and the automated workflows that define the platform experience. The company's integrations with QuickBooks, Xero, and major banking partners provide additional data dimensions that few competitors can match.

Agent Mel — Melio's AI agent for automated AP/AR — has made data science a first-class strategic function at the company. Data Scientists contribute the predictive models, anomaly detection systems, and cash-flow forecasting algorithms that power Agent Mel's autonomous decision-making. When Agent Mel recommends optimal payment timing, flags unusual vendor invoices, or predicts a cash-flow shortfall, it relies on models built and maintained by the data science team. The SMB Empowerment premium means these models must be accurate enough to earn the trust of small business owners who are entrusting their financial operations to an AI agent — automating repetitive cash-flow tasks requires precision that eliminates, rather than creates, anxiety.

Melio compensates data scientists with options on a 4-year vesting schedule, reflecting its private company status (backed by Accel, Bessemer, and Coatue at a peak $4B valuation). Candidates from public tech companies should factor in the illiquidity discount but also consider that Melio's data environment — $100B+ in real payment data — provides career-accelerating experience that is difficult to replicate elsewhere. The combination of AI agent development and financial data science creates a uniquely valuable skill portfolio.
