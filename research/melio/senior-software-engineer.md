**Negotiation DNA**

The Senior Software Engineer role at Melio is where individual contributors begin to shape platform-level decisions that impact the company's $100B+ payment processing infrastructure. At this level, you are expected to own complete feature domains — from architecting the integration layer that connects 500,000+ SMBs to their QuickBooks and Xero accounts, to designing the real-time payment routing systems that enable free ACH transfers at scale. Melio's partnerships with major banks require engineers who can navigate complex compliance requirements while shipping rapidly, and senior engineers are the primary talent pool that makes this possible.

The introduction of Agent Mel — Melio's AI agent for automated AP/AR — has elevated the strategic importance of senior engineers dramatically. Agent Mel requires sophisticated orchestration of payment workflows, natural language understanding for invoice processing, and intelligent decision-making for cash-flow optimization. Senior Software Engineers are expected to lead the technical implementation of these capabilities, making this role central to Melio's 2026 product strategy. The SMB Empowerment premium recognizes that automating repetitive cash-flow tasks for small businesses is not merely a product feature but a competitive moat that senior engineers build and defend.

Compensation at this level reflects Melio's positioning as a top-tier New York fintech competing for talent against Stripe, Block, and Bill.com. As a private company backed by Accel, Bessemer, and Coatue at a peak $4B valuation, Melio offers options on a 4-year vesting schedule. Candidates should evaluate the spread between the current 409A valuation and the last preferred price — the $100B+ processing milestone and Agent Mel launch suggest meaningful upside potential, but illiquidity remains a factor that justifies negotiating higher base compensation or additional equity.
