**Negotiation DNA**

Staff Software Engineers at Nuvei sit at the architectural decision layer that determines whether Advent International's $6.3 billion investment generates target returns. At this level, you are not simply writing code — you are defining the technical strategy for a payment platform that processes transactions across 200+ markets with 700+ alternative payment methods, maintains Electronic Currency Issuer compliance across multiple regulatory jurisdictions, and holds e-money licenses that require continuous engineering oversight. The staff-level role at a PE-backed company carries unique leverage: Advent needs architects who can make platform decisions that compound value over the hold period, and the cost of architectural mistakes at this scale is measured in hundreds of millions.

The Pay2All acquisition in Brazil exemplifies the type of strategic technical challenge that lands on staff engineers' desks. Integrating a Brazilian payment platform into Nuvei's global infrastructure requires decisions about data sovereignty, real-time currency conversion architecture, compliance automation, and API contract design that will shape the company's Latin American business for the next decade. Staff engineers who can navigate these decisions while maintaining Nuvei's i-Gaming vertical leadership — where real-time transaction processing, fraud detection, and regulatory compliance intersect — are extraordinarily scarce in the market.

At the staff level, your negotiation is fundamentally about architectural scarcity. There are perhaps a few hundred engineers globally who have both the payment infrastructure expertise and the regulatory domain knowledge to architect Electronic Currency Issuer systems at Nuvei's scale. Advent International understands this scarcity premium because they've seen it across their portfolio of PE-backed technology companies. Your compensation should reflect that you are one of the highest-leverage individuals in the entire organization, with options vesting over four years that could generate substantial returns at Advent's eventual exit.
