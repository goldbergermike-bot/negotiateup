#### Negotiation DNA

Security Engineers at SoFi protect a chartered bank, publicly traded financial platform, and enterprise BaaS provider -- a scope of security responsibility that rivals the largest financial institutions. With $1B quarterly revenue, 10M+ members, and 130M+ Galileo accounts, the attack surface and regulatory security requirements are immense. Security Engineers are expected to operate across application security, infrastructure security, fraud prevention, identity management, and regulatory compliance (OCC, SOC 2, PCI-DSS, GLBA).

SoFi's publicly traded status (NASDAQ: SOFI, ~$15B+ market cap) means security incidents have immediate market consequences -- a data breach or fraud event would affect stock price, regulatory standing, and member trust simultaneously. This makes Security Engineers among the most strategically critical hires in the organization. The 30% growth outlook amplifies this demand: every new product, member, and enterprise client expands the attack surface that security engineers must defend.

Security Engineers protect and enable the LTV acceleration engine:

1. **Trust = LTV Foundation:** In financial services, security is the foundation of member trust, and trust is the foundation of lifetime value. A single security incident can destroy years of accumulated member LTV across 10M+ accounts. Security engineers who maintain a clean security posture protect billions in aggregate member lifetime value -- this protective function is the ultimate LTV lever.

2. **Compliance as Competitive Advantage:** SoFi operates under a bank charter with OCC oversight. Security engineers who maintain compliance with banking security regulations don't just avoid penalties -- they enable SoFi to offer products (like FDIC-insured accounts) that non-chartered fintechs cannot. Compliance is a competitive moat, and security engineers are the ones who build and maintain it.

3. **Galileo Enterprise Security:** Enterprise clients choose Galileo BaaS based partly on its security posture. Security engineers who can demonstrate SOC 2 Type II compliance, robust access controls, and comprehensive threat monitoring directly enable enterprise sales. Each enterprise contract represents $1M-$10M+ in annual revenue that depends on security credibility.

4. **Fraud Prevention as Revenue Protection:** Security engineers building fraud detection and prevention systems directly protect revenue. In lending, preventing fraudulent applications saves millions in default losses. In banking, preventing account takeover protects member balances and trust. The ROI of fraud prevention engineering is measurable in direct dollar terms.

Frame the negotiation: "As a Security Engineer at SoFi, I protect the trust foundation that makes member lifetime value possible. Every day without a security incident across 10M+ members and 130M+ Galileo accounts is a day I've protected billions in aggregate LTV."
