**Negotiation DNA**

Product Designers at Bill.com are solving one of the hardest UX problems in B2B fintech: making complex financial automation feel simple for 475,000+ businesses. The Agentic AP/AR Automation platform introduces AI-driven workflows — autonomous invoice processing, intelligent approval routing, predictive cash flow management — that must be transparent, trustworthy, and usable by finance teams who are not technical users. Your design decisions directly determine adoption rates, and adoption rates directly drive Revenue-Cycle growth across the $2.6T mid-market B2B opportunity.

The design surface at Bill.com extends across multiple product lines: the core AP/AR platform, Divvy's spend management experience, and Invoice2go's invoicing tools. Each product serves different user personas with different levels of financial sophistication, yet they must all feel like a coherent financial back-office automation suite. This cross-product design coherence challenge is what makes Bill.com's Product Designer role particularly valuable — and particularly hard to fill with designers who have genuine B2B financial workflow experience.

Bill.com's $1.3B+ revenue validates that the platform works, but the Agentic AP transition introduces entirely new design paradigms: how do users interact with AI agents making financial decisions? How do you design trust into autonomous payment workflows? These are frontier design problems, and designers who can solve them command a Revenue-Cycle premium in their compensation package.
