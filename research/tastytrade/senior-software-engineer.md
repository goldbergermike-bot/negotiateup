#### Negotiation DNA

At the Senior Software Engineer level, Tastytrade expects you to own significant portions of their derivatives-native platform — from real-time options pricing engines to multi-leg strategy builders that serve millions of active traders. This is not a typical "senior" role at a generic fintech; you are architecting systems that must compute and display Greeks in real time, model volatility surfaces across thousands of options contracts, and execute complex multi-leg orders with institutional-grade reliability. The depth of domain knowledge required at this level creates a natural moat around your negotiating position.

IG Group's $1B acquisition of Tastytrade has elevated the senior engineering mandate. You are now expected to drive integration between Tastytrade's options-native architecture and IG Group's global multi-asset platform spanning FX, CFDs, and spread betting. Senior engineers who can navigate both ecosystems — who understand the differences between equity options market microstructure and FX derivatives conventions — are extraordinarily rare. This cross-platform fluency is a lever you should press hard during negotiations.

Tom Sosnoff's vision for Tastytrade has always been about making high-probability options trading accessible. At the senior level, you are the engineer who translates that vision into production systems — building features that embody concepts like probability of profit, expected move, and portfolio-level Greeks management. Your ability to operate at the intersection of quantitative finance and scalable software architecture is what makes this role command premium compensation above standard senior SWE roles in fintech.

#### Options Alpha — The Derivatives-Native Premium

At the Senior Software Engineer level, the Derivatives-Native premium becomes significantly more impactful because you are making architectural decisions that directly affect how the platform handles options pricing, risk computation, and strategy execution at scale.

- **The Derivatives-Native Bonus (8-15% above standard fintech):** Senior engineers with demonstrated options/derivatives expertise command a Derivatives-Native bonus of $17K-$46K above standard senior SWE compensation at comparable fintech firms. This premium — 8-15% of total comp — reflects your ability to architect systems around vol surfaces, Greeks grids, and multi-leg options order routing. At the senior level, this typically manifests as a $18K-$28K base uplift and a $8K-$18K annual bonus uplift, plus potential for accelerated RSU grants tied to derivatives platform milestones.

- **High-Probability Trading Focus:** Tastytrade's competitive moat is its focus on high-probability trading — strategies with defined risk, favorable probability of profit, and manageable Greeks exposure. Senior engineers must deeply understand these concepts to make sound architectural decisions. When you design a system that calculates real-time probability of profit across 50,000 active positions, or that renders interactive P&L curves for complex spread strategies, you are applying derivatives domain knowledge that most senior engineers simply do not possess. Frame every architecture decision in your interview and negotiation through this Options Alpha lens.

- **IG Group Global Integration:** The integration of Tastytrade with IG Group's global platform is the defining engineering challenge of 2025-2027. Senior engineers are leading the effort to unify options pricing models with IG's FX and CFD pricing infrastructure, harmonize risk management systems across regulatory jurisdictions (SEC/CFTC in the US, FCA in the UK), and build cross-product analytics that span equity options, FX options, and structured products. Each of these integration workstreams requires derivatives-native engineering that commands premium compensation.

- **tastylive Media Network Synergy:** At the senior level, you may architect systems that connect Tastytrade's trading platform with the tastylive media network — enabling features like live trade alerts linked to educational content, real-time strategy backtesting integrated with market commentary, and social trading features that surface high-probability setups. This content-platform convergence is unique to Tastytrade and requires senior engineers who understand both the media distribution challenge and the derivatives domain.
